About MIT-WPU

MIT World Peace University (MIT-WPU), located in Pune, India, is one of the country’s premier institutions of higher education, known for combining academic excellence with values of peace, sustainability, and innovation. Established under the umbrella of the MIT Group of Institutions (founded in 1983), MIT-WPU stands out for its unique blend of modern education and ancient Indian wisdom, committed to nurturing not only skilled professionals but also responsible global citizens—a strong emphasis on Peace Studies, Value-based Learning, and Social Impact.

At MIT World Peace University (MIT-WPU), sustainability is not just a concept—it's a core philosophy woven into our academic, research, and operational frameworks. Guided by the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), MIT-WPU is committed to nurturing environmentally conscious global citizens who contribute to a more equitable, peaceful, and sustainable world.

Our Vision for Sustainability

Rooted in the principles of Peace, Innovation, and Social Responsibility, MIT-WPU strives to be a leader in higher education that actively contributes to global sustainability challenges through education, research, community engagement, and institutional practices.

About SDG Goals

17 Goals to Transform Our World

The Sustainable Development Goals are a call for action by all countries – poor, rich and middle-income – to promote prosperity while protecting the planet. They recognize that ending poverty must go hand-in-hand with strategies that build economic growth and address a range of social needs including education, health, social protection, and job opportunities, while tackling climate change and environmental protection.

MIT-WPU has received rankings from bodies like NIRF, QS, and Times Higher Education, The World University Raking for Innovation and these rankings often consider aspects related to sustainability and social impact, indirectly reflecting its commitment to SDGs.
