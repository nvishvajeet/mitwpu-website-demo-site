Women Empowerment

Women’s Day Event

The Women’s Day Event held on 10th March 2025 at MIT-WPU aligned with SDG 5 (Gender Equality) and SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities), celebrating women’s achievements in STEM and promoting gender equity. Through engaging panel discussions and interactive showcases, the event highlighted the contributions of women leaders, inspired young minds, and reinforced the university’s commitment to fostering inclusivity, empowerment, and equal opportunities across all domains.

Pratibha Excellence Award from Eaton

MIT World Peace University is proud to highlight the achievement of Ananya Shimpi, who received the prestigious Pratibha Excellence Award from Eaton. The national-level competition saw participation from over 100 students across India, including 40 from MIT-WPU’s School of Engineering. After multiple selection rounds, only 16 students were declared winners. Ananya’s recognition underscores the university’s commitment to empowering women in engineering and fostering excellence, innovation, and leadership among its students. pdf report attached in drive Events folder named Women

Women Entrepreneur Track – HackMIT-WPU

At MIT World Peace University, women-led teams are empowered to drive innovation and leadership in technology and entrepreneurship. SLDP

MIT-WPU hosted the Social Leadership Development Programme (SLDP) 2025 from August 6-8, bringing together social leaders, innovators, and students to focus on community-driven change. The three-day programme featured experts from various fields sharing practical insights on leadership, environmental conservation, entrepreneurship, sustainable development and more.

Rural Immersion

Rural immersion programmes by MIT-WPU are a unique, one-of-a-kind learning activity meticulously designed to sensitize the students to the lifestyle of the rural community and train them to apply their knowledge, modern technology and management concepts to find solutions to problems faced by the rural communities.

Community Engagement

Many events are conducted to promote community engagement through various oriented outreach programs and social initiatives. Students and faculty collaborate with local communities to address social challenges. Some of the glimpse are as

The Bytes4Charity Hackathon is an event that combines technological innovation with a heart for social impact. The hackathon's challenge is both exciting and purpose-driven: to design a website for a non-profit organization (NGO) dedicated to a meaningful social cause.

CSI MIT-WPU’s Tech Trivia Challenge , held on International Women’s Day, was a high-energy, 4-minute quiz testing participants' tech knowledge. Hosted using ICT tool Kahoot, it attracted enthusiastic participants eager to compete for exciting prizes, creating an engaging, fast-paced, and intellectually stimulating experience for all involved.

NGO Visit aimed to create awareness about cybercrime and explain what exactly the internet world looks like. We explained to the students the dual nature of the online world, its advantages, and potential risks. This session was highly interactive, as the students candidly shared their internet usage habits, revealing their inclination for extended gaming sessions including popular titles like BGMI and Freefire.

Cyber Awareness Session: Cybersecurity awareness session dedicated to empowering senior citizens was conducted by Dr. Aditi jahagirdar and Dr. Dhanashree Wategaonkar, faculty members of Department of Computer Engineering and Technology on 23rd July 2025. It is very well received and appreciated by the senior citizens as it is the need of the hour to remain cautious and alert.
