The Curriculum at MIT-WPU

The curriculum for engineering courses at MIT-WPU is meticulously crafted to provide students with a holistic development beyond academics. Here are the key components:

1. Hands-on training in technologies and tools: Students engage in practical sessions to gain proficiency in the latest technologies and tools relevant to their field.

2. Industry visits, guest lectures, seminars, and workshops: Eminent researchers and industry practitioners regularly interact with students, offering insights into real-world applications and industry trends.

3. Dedicated Centre for Industry-Academia Partnerships: A specialised centre facilitates internship and placement assistance, fostering collaborations between academia and industry.

4. Skill enhancement courses: Additional courses are offered to enhance students' skill sets, catering to the evolving needs of the job market.

5. Encouraging entrepreneurship: MIT-WPU supports entrepreneurial endeavours through funding, mentoring, and networking opportunities, empowering students to pursue innovative ideas.

6. MoUs with 231+ corporates: Partnerships with leading corporates provide avenues for training, research, and development, enriching students' learning experiences.

7. Six-month industry internship: Students gain valuable work experience through a mandatory six-month industry internship, bridging the gap between theory and practice.

8. National and international immersion programmes: Immersion programmes expose students to diverse cultural and professional environments, broadening their perspectives.

9. Case-based learning pedagogy: Learning is facilitated through real-life case studies, encouraging the development of critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

10. State-of-the-art lab facilities: Well-equipped laboratories offer students hands-on experience with cutting-edge technology, facilitating practical learning and experimentation.
