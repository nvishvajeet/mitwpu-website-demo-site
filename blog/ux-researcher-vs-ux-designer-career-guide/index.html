UX Researchers vs UX Designers Salary: A Career Guide for Design Students 2026

Discover the salary difference between UX Researchers and UX Designers in 2026. Compare skills, responsibilities, career growth, and job opportunities to choose the right design career.

The yearly salary of a UX designer is ₹6-15 LPA; however, a UX researcher earns ₹7.5-19 LPA in India. Although the careers of UX designers and UX researchers are growing exponentially, both roles require different tools, skill sets, and mindsets. If you enjoy creating interfaces and prototypes, then UX design is your way to fast-track your career. And if you are interested in psychology, human interaction, and data handling, then you are perfect for a UX researcher role.

UX Researcher vs UX Designer Salary: Understanding the Job Roles

The job of a UX Researcher is closely related to dealing with human psychology, where their job is to know ‘Why users are struggling to navigate through a particular interface?’ They obtain this information through taking interviews, online surveys, and tests to get clear directions.

On the other hand, the job of the UX Designer is to act on the researched material and find answers to ‘How can I make the user navigate through the app without getting lost?’ They actually create the framework of the interface, prototypes, and a flow of interaction that translates user insights into an actual product experience.

In simple words, the researcher finds the problems and the designer finds creative ways to solve them. These are two different roles in large companies like Flipkart, Amazon, Swiggy, etc., but in small companies, the roles are not clearly defined, so the differentiating lines are blurry.

Why do Senior UX Researchers Earn More than Senior UX Designers in India?

The reason why the UX Researcher is getting paid more is that the job requires fair knowledge of psychology, statistics, and, most importantly, people’s skills. There are very few people who have the talent of synthesising hundreds of interviews to come to one conclusion for product direction. That scarcity drives the pay for the job.

Skills Required for UX Roles

Must-Have Skills for the UX Researcher

  • Organising and conducting User Interviews
  • Surveying the design and analysis of it
  • Testing usability, both moderated and unmoderated
  • Mapping the affinity and synthesis
  • Data Interpretation and simple statistical analysis

Must-Have Skills for the UX Designer:

  • Prototyping and Wireframing
  • Creating a visual hierarchy and digital layouts
  • Checking the interaction design principles
  • Understanding of the design system
  • Developers’ collaboration
  • Storytelling
  • Communication, both verbal and presentation
  • Cross-functional collaboration

Necessary Tools for UX Creators

For UX Researchers: Maze, User Testing, Lookback, Dovetail, Airtable, Google Forms, Typeform, and Micro—for synthesis.

For UX Designers: Figma, Adobe XD, Sketch, Principle, ProtoPie, Zeplin, and InVision are some essential tools to be hands-on.

Career Growth in the UX Field

Although both paths branch differently, they have strong growth trajectories initially.

For example, UX researchers start with Junior Researcher, then come UX Researcher, followed by Senior Researcher, Research Lead, Head of Lead, or UX Strategy. And for a UX Designer, it goes like: Junior Designer followed by UX Designer, Senior Designer, Design Lead, then Head of Design or Product Design Director.

The research lead is often seen moving to product strategy or UX consulting roles as they move forward. And designers tend to move into product management or design leadership roles. Both paths at the senior levels of leadership can reach up to ₹30–45 LPA in India.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Which role pays a higher salary – UX Researcher or UX Designer? At the entry level, the salary for both roles ranges from ₹4–7.5 LPA. However, at the senior level, a UX Designer earns ₹15-20 LPA, while a UX Researcher earns definitely more, around ₹18-27 LPA

Are these roles of UX Designer and UX Researcher interchangeable in the future? YES! This seems like a common move in the Indian industry. If the skills required for being a researcher are developed by a designer, like building strong user testing and people skills, then it is very much possible to interchange the career path at any point in time.

Is a UX Researcher a good career move in India in 2026? YES! The demand for dedicated roles for UX Researcher is increasing since the growth of product-led companies, fintech, and edtech is increasing rapidly in India. Research is no longer a responsibility for a designer; tech-leading companies like Meesho, Google Pay, Myntra, etc., have separate teams of UX Researchers, with the sole purpose of research.

Which degree is required for a UX career in India? A B.Des in UX Design, a bachelor’s programme, is enough for you to start your career in UX in India. MIT-WPU, Pune, provides a B.Des programme that includes skills and tools for you to prepare for both career lines that the industry is hiring.
