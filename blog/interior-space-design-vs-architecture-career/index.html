Interior and Space Design vs Architecture: Which Career Path Should You Choose?

Compare interior and space design vs. architecture careers based on salary, skills, course structure, creativity, and future job opportunities.

Reconstructed from the retained MIT-WPU public capture. View official source

We are all emotionally attached to some or the other place, be it our home, office, cafes, or even hotels... We feel calm, inspired, peaceful, and free because of the thoughtful design of these spaces. Behind this transformation is the creative expertise of the space designers.

However, people often get confused between an architect and an interior & space designer. Although both professions are related to designing spaces, the two fields are different from each other in terms of education, career opportunities, and responsibilities.

Before we discuss the difference between Interior & Space Design and Architecture, let us learn more about both streams in detail.

Introduction to Interior & Space Design

Interior and space design is the art of aesthetically decorating a space, without compromising the comfort and purpose of the space. It focuses on aesthetics, comfort, and purpose—selecting color palettes, furniture, lighting, and decor for that perfect finish.

Interior and space design incorporates the following key components :

  • Colour schemes and sustainable materials
  • Furniture, décor and layout optimisation
  • Lighting design
  • Acoustic planning
  • Space flow and functionality

Core Job Role of an Interior and Space Designer

The interior & space designer is primarily crafting the inside story of a space by prioritising visual appeal, textures, mood and comfort of occupant through furnishings and layout

Skills Required to be an Interior and Space Designer

  • Blend of technical and artistic abilities
  • Strong creative and visualisation skills
  • Effective Communication abilities
  • Knowledge of technical tools like 3D Modelling and CAD
  • Accurate understanding of lighting, material, mood, and design trends
  • Creating detailed sketches & layout plans using 3D software
  • Working within budget and project deadlines

Core Career Opportunities in Interior and Space Design

  • Commercial Interior Designer
  • Hospitality Designer
  • Set/ Production Designer
  • Furniture Designer
  • Interior Stylist
  • Residential Space Designer

and many more….

The programme equips students with advanced technologies such as Green building practices and Virtual Reality. It also enables them to work with 3D modelling, digital rendering, and material selection.

  • Digital Drawing
  • Retail Design

Introduction to Architecture

Architecture is about designing buildings and spaces where people live, work, and gather. It combines creativity, practicality, and culture to make a structure useful, safe, and functional. Though Architecture uses some of the techniques of civil engineering, it is considered a visual art, as it deals with both the process and product of planning, conceiving, designing, and structuring.

The main job of an architect is to design the layout of a building or structure appropriately. Their work begins before actual construction begins and continues until the project is completed.

  • To understand the needs of the client and prepare blueprints, technical designs, and architectural plans accordingly
  • To ensure that the work done complies with all norms & regulations, and building codes
  • Coordinate with engineers, contractors, and builders
  • Site visit and supervise the construction process
  • Both fields are closely connected to the concept of space and built environments
  • They combine elements of art, creativity, and technical knowledge
  • Professionals in both fields require strong design thinking and innovation
  • People in both sectors must coordinate with builders, engineers, and contractors and work accordingly.
  • Both need to stay updated on new trends in their respective fields.
  • Whether you are an architect or an interior designer, you should be extremely creative and passionate in your work.

Differences between Architecture and Interior, and Space Design

An architect is primarily responsible for designing and developing the structure of the spaces, whereas an interior and space designer looks after the aesthetics and interiors of the space.

Final Thoughts

Interior & Space Design and Architecture share some of the fundamental similarities as well as distinctive differences.

Choosing among architecture, interior design, and spatial design depends on your interests, career goals, and personality. If we think beyond similarities and differences, the fact is that both fields can give new dimensions to your creativity and offer a tremendous level of work satisfaction.

No matter which of these two fields you choose, there are many great career opportunities available for you in the future.
