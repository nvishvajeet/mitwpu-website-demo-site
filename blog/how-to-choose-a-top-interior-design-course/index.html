How to Choose a Top Interior Design Course

Interior design is one of the top courses in India since it involves lots of creativity and imagination. With India rethinking the interiors of all major structures like airports, this is one of the lucrative careers for the future.

Interior design is one of the top courses in India since it involves lots of creativity and imagination. With India rethinking the interiors of all major structures like airports, this is one of the lucrative careers for the future. To hone your skills in the designing industry, and if you are looking for a college to join in Pune for interior design, choose MIT-WPU ! Before that, understand how to filter colleges to study interior design.

Factors to Consider when Choosing an Interior Design Course

Accreditation and recognition:

  • Ensure that the top regional educational board accredits your interior design course. This establishes that the course can be recognized for validation for your career. For example, B. Des designs are approved by the UGC in India. Check this before applying for any college.
  • You also need to verify the reputation of the course and college. If you can discuss it with some seniors or alumni, you will understand better how the college will help you mold a career in interior design.

Faculty expertise and experience:

  • All courses demand faculty who understands how industries work outside the college and syllabus. It’s the same for interior design. This field evolves with the latest trends, materials, and resources available in the market. If your syllabus and faculty are not updated, you won’t be able to keep up with your peers.
  • Assess the teaching style. See if the course is taught based on projects or theory and which method works the best for you. Also, verify if the faculty holds relevant degrees on the college website.

Course curriculum and modules:

  • Scrutinize the course content. See if it covers the latest interior design principles, skills, and software. These are a few common topics that every B. Des courses should cover:
  • Interior design history and digital literacy
  • Color theory and lighting designs.
  • Sustainable design practices
  • Business management
  • Building codes and safety regulations

What if you want to channel your interest in any of these specializations – residential, commercial, or healthcare interior design? Does your college have these in the courses they offer? Think before finalizing.

Here are some additional points to keep in mind:

  • Location of the college and teaching method: Have you decided whether to attend college full-time, part-time, or online? If full-time, consider whether you should commute daily or stay in a hostel if it’s far away from your residence.
  • Class size and student-teacher ratio: You can’t study in a class with 60 students with only one professor to guide you. So, choose a college that has more faculty for a set of students.
  • Cost and financial aid options: Compare tuition fees and scholarships to find the interior course that fits your budget.
  • Career support: Does the interior design course you chose offer plenty of opportunities to develop a portfolio for your career while still at college? These should be the factors to consider before choosing a course and college.

Types of interior design

Interior design for all spaces may be the same. No, it’s unique! The number of specializations is diverse.

Residential design:

  • Focuses on creating highly functional, creative, and aesthetically pleasing living spaces for houses, apartments, penthouses, farmhouses, and more. The specialized areas in this setting include kitchen, bedroom, and living room designs.

Commercial Design:

  • Commercial designing includes office spaces, retail stores, restaurants, hotels, and other commercial settings. The specialized areas include hospitality, healthcare, workplace, and shopping complex designs.

Contract Design:

  • In contract designs, your clients will be architects, developers, and contractors on large-scale projects like hotels, office buildings, and hospitals. As a designer, you will work under project management, budgeting, and space planning teams.

Exhibition Design:

  • You will get experience designing museums, trade shows, and other exhibition spaces. The primary factors involved in this design will be visual communication, technology integration, and interactive designs.

Sustainable Design:

  • Emphasizes the use of eco-friendly materials, energy-efficient practices, and responsible resource management, especially when it’s a gated community.

Specializations and focus areas:

When choosing an interior design path, keep in mind your specific interests and strengths since you have:

  • Space planning and layout: Optimising a space's flow, look, and functionality after development.
  • Furniture and fixture selection: Choosing furniture and other things like art and pottery adds value and aesthetics.
  • Color theory and lighting design: Creating a certain vibe and mood for a place with colors and lights.
  • Material selection : Choosing aesthetically pleasing, durable, and sustainable materials.
  • Building codes and safety regulations: Ensuring the creation of places that are safe to live, shop, or work.
  • Project management: Coordinating all aspects of a design project from conception to completion.

Before choosing a B. Des Interior Design course , check how many factors comply with your choice. B. Des at MIT-WPU ensures you are adept with concepts like space transformation, spatial ergonomics, and more, which will influence you to become a top interior designer!

FAQs

How do I choose the right specialization in Interior Design?

What do you prefer? Designing for homes, hospitals, or commercial spaces? Based on this, choose your specialization.

What qualifications do you need for the interior design course?

You should have passed 10+2 with at least 65% or must have passed any qualifying exams like MIT-WPU CET 2024.

How can I do an interior design course after the 12th?

You can choose to pursue a B. Des or a diploma in interior design after the 12th grade.

Is there any competitive exam for interior design?

It depends on the college you choose. There is an entrance to join MIT-WPU, named the MIT-WPU CET. Or, you will have to appear for exams conducted by the National Institute of Design.

What career support services do top Interior Design courses offer?

You can start your interior design studio or partner with top architectural firms for project-based roles after B. Des.
