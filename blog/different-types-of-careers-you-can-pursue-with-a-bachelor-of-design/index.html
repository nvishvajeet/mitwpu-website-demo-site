Different Types of Careers You Can Pursue with a Bachelor of Design

A Bachelor of Design opens up a world of creative careers. Explore the different paths you can pursue, from UX/UI to product design.

Reconstructed from the retained MIT-WPU public capture. View official source

With the rapid evolution of industries and technologies, the demand for skilled designers has surged across various sectors. A Bachelor of Design degree offers a gateway to many career opportunities where creativity meets functionality. At MIT-WPU , students are equipped with comprehensive knowledge and practical skills to thrive in the dynamic world of design.

Scopes After Bachelors in Design Course

After completing a Bachelor of Design course, graduates have many career prospects in various industries. They can pursue roles such as graphic designer, interior designer, product designer, fashion designer, game designer, exhibition designer, and more. With their creative skills and technical knowledge, they can contribute to fields ranging from advertising and marketing to architecture and entertainment.

Additionally, design professionals have opportunities to work in diverse settings, including design studios, corporate firms, freelance projects, and entrepreneurial ventures. The versatility of a design degree allows graduates to explore their passions and make meaningful contributions to society through innovative design solutions.

Popular Design Careers after Bachelor of Design Course

  • Graphics Designer: Graphics designers create visual concepts using computer software to communicate ideas that inspire, inform, or captivate consumers. They work on various projects, such as advertisements, websites, and branding materials.
  • Interior Designer: Interior designers plan and design spaces to enhance functionality and aesthetics. They collaborate with clients to create environments that meet their needs and reflect their style preferences.
  • Product Designer: Product designers conceptualise and develop innovative products that improve people's lives. They combine artistic flair with technical knowledge to design products ranging from consumer goods to industrial equipment.
  • Fashion Designer: Fashion designers design clothing and accessories, interpret trends, and create unique garments for individuals or mass production. They possess a keen understanding of fabrics, colours, and styles to craft visually appealing collections.
  • Game Designer: Game designers conceptualise and develop interactive experiences for various platforms, including video games, mobile apps, and virtual reality simulations. They design gameplay mechanics, levels, and narratives to engage players and create memorable experiences.
  • Exhibition Designer: Exhibition designers create immersive environments for events, museums, and trade shows. They design layouts, displays, and interactive elements to enhance visitor experiences and convey key messages effectively.

Expected Salary after B. Des Course

Salaries for Bachelor of Design graduates vary depending on factors such as experience, specialisation, and location. Entry-level positions in design typically offer salaries ranging from INR 4 to INR 7 lakhs per year. With experience and expertise, professionals can earn significantly higher salaries, with some senior designers commanding six-figure incomes. Moreover, freelancing and entrepreneurship offer opportunities for designers to increase their earning potential based on project fees and business success.

A Bachelor of Design degree opens doors to various rewarding career paths. Whether you're passionate about creating visual art, shaping spaces, or designing innovative products, a B. Des degree at MIT-WPU offers endless opportunities for creativity and impact. With the right skills and determination, graduates can embark on fulfilling journeys in the dynamic world of design.
