Mergers & Acquisitions Jobs: What MBA Finance Recruiters Expect You to Know

Insider look at mergers & acquisitions hiring - the technical and strategic skills recruiters silently screen for in MBA finance candidates. Read more.

Among the highest-stakes and most rewarding career options, mergers and acquisitions jobs in India are a specialised category in the world of finance. And there is cutthroat competition to get hired for the roles offered in this domain. This blog will guide you if you are an MBA finance student or someone aiming to get into an M&A profile. You will get to know what the industry experts have to say about the working and the hiring strategy of this domain. You will also get to know some tips about how to put yourself ahead of the competition in this industry.

What is a Mergers and Acquisitions Job in India?

By the definition in the book, mergers and acquisitions (M&A) is a process that helps two or more companies from the same or different industries to combine (or merge) in order to work together as a team. In this, at times one large company purchases another company/companies (acquisition) to meet its core strategic goal. These mergers and acquisitions happen for multiple professional reasons, like expanding market share, acquiring new technologies, entering new geographical markets, or even eliminating competition.

​In India, you will find that these mergers and acquisitions jobs are a combination of finance, strategy, and law backgrounds. The person working in this field will need to advise companies on structuring the deal strategically, ensuring and conducting the financial due diligence, creating valuation models, coming up with negotiating terms, and ensuring the regulatory compliance of the company.

Fast-growing sectors like technology, finance, infrastructure, medicines & healthcare demand skilled, talented and seasoned M&A professionals, as India is one of the emerging and most-active deal markets. M&A jobs in India represent one of the most financially rewarding as well as most intellectually challenging jobs for MBA finance graduates.

Career Opportunities for Freshers in the M&A Industry

The entry point into the M&A ecosystem is across all investment banks; advisory firms of the Big Four, like Deloitte, EY, KPMG, and PwC; private equity firms; boutique deal advisory firms; and large corporate development firms. Understanding where you stand in these is the first step you should be taking.

Top Job Roles and Careers in M&A

  • M&A Analyst in Investment Banking – This is the very basic entry-level and well-known role for M&A in the banking sector. You might have to work on analysis and building financial models, create a pitch deck, and support due diligence, as well as work directly on the live transactions happening.
  • Transaction Advisory Associate – The professionals in this field have to conduct financial due diligence for buyers and sellers, identify risks, and validate the financial accounts before the deal closes.
  • Corporate Development Analyst – This role is in-housed in large companies and involves identifying the talent the company wants to acquire, strategising internal deal analysis, and managing all the post-merger integration formalities. This role offers the strategic edge that pure banking roles don't offer.
  • Private Equity Analyst (PE Analyst) – They evaluate the portfolio investment of potential clients, help in building the LBO models, and also monitor the acquired companies.
  • M&A Legal Specialist and Compliance – This role is more suitable for those who have dual qualifications in finance and law. In the field of M&A, the roles for regulation and compliance are growing rapidly, considering the complexity of cross-border transactions.

Framework for M&A Career Launch

To make a career in the M&A domain, you need to work on the three fundamental pillars :

  • Foundation Skills include core financial knowledge, modelling, valuation, etc. If you are pursuing a degree in finance, then you will get an overall idea of building a financial model.
  • Deal Immersion involves live case studies, M&A simulations and exposure to transaction workshops in which the whole lifecycle of a merger is explained to you with mock-up cases.
  • Industry Connects through internships and by using professional networking platforms like LinkedIn to develop industry connections.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs):

Where do you find mergers and acquisitions jobs in India? Ans. For Mergers and Acquisitions jobs in India, an M&A Analyst is going to be an initial role at investment banks. Or you can start with a Transaction Advisory Associates position at the Big Four firms. With strong financial modelling skills, an MBA graduate has a greater chance of getting hired.

What is the merger & acquisition salary in India at the entry-level? Ans. An entry-level M&A professional can get ₹8 lakh to ₹25 LPA, including base compensation and bonuses, depending on the employer. Investment banks in Mumbai offer the highest package as compared to the corporate roles offered in Pune. However, corporates in Pune offer more structured growth with slightly lower salaries initially.

What does a working day in the life of M&A look like? Building financial models, preparing pitch decks, conducting target research, and conducting due diligence processes are the normal challenges. Apart from that, they have to coordinate with the legal team, tax team, and compliance team, which is another side of the coin. Most of the time, the role requires working long hours, precision in decision-making, and the ability to multitask.

Do I need an MBA in Finance to get jobs in Mergers and Acquisitions in India? Ans. Your access to jobs in Mergers and Acquisitions increases when you have an MBA degree in finance from a reputable college. Like MIT-WPU, Pune provides an MBA in Finance with lots of practical experience, internships and industry exposure at a very early stage.
