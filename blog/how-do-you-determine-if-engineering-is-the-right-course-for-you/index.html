How do you determine if engineering is the right course for you?

Engineering is a challenging course that needs lots of focus, determination, and skills to complete and gain a job.

Reconstructed from the retained MIT-WPU public capture. View official source

Choosing a university course is a big step. You might hear friends rave about coding, while family members urge you to pick civil or mechanical. You could also be wondering, Is engineering the right course for me?, or Should I follow an entirely different path?

This guide dissects the decision into easy-to-follow checkpoints - skills, interests, job trends and costs — so you can make a decision with confidence.

ii. Do a Self-Assessment Quiz: “Is Engineering for Me?”

Get yourself a pen and paper — or notepad app would also work — and score each statement from 1 (not at all like me) to 5 (a whole lot like me) :

1. I like tinkering with gadgets, or trying to figure out how things are done.

2. For me, maths problems are puzzles, not torture.

3. When I spot traffic congestion or blackouts, I think about how to address these problems.

4. I can see the trees without losing sight of the forest.

5. I find team projects to be more attractive than single essays.

6. I’m curious about emerging tech—AI, renewable energy, and space missions.

7. I like turning ideas into physical models or working apps.

Add your scores.

  • 30+: Engineering could be a natural fit.
  • 20-29: You might succeed, but explore specific branches before deciding.
  • Below 20: Consider other careers, or choose a hybrid field such as design or management.

Repeat the quiz after shadowing an engineer for a day or attending a college open house—it keeps your answer for — is engineering the right course for me? — grounded in real experience.

iv. Latest Job-Market Trends

  • Graduate salaries in core tech roles have risen 8-12 % yearly since 2021.
  • Remote engineering work—especially software and CAD—now accounts for roughly 25 % of entry-level jobs.
  • Government pushes for “Make in India” and green energy fuel demand for electronics, chemical, and renewable energy engineers.

If you’re asking again, is engineering the right course for me? Those numbers suggest steady demand, provided you keep skills up to date.

vi. How to Choose the Right Engineering Branch

1. Match interests: Love coding? Computer streams suit you. Enjoy chemistry practicals? Think chemical or materials.

2. Check aptitude: Strong in drawing? Civil or mechanical design might click. Quick with statistics? Data science feels natural.

3. Shadow professionals: Spend a day with a family friend in each branch. Real-world observation beats glossy brochures.

4. Review curricula: Some courses merge disciplines—e.g., Electronics & Communication (AI and ML). Pick one that excites you every semester, not just in year four.

5. Look at exit routes: Research typical recruiters, higher-study pathways, and industry clusters in your preferred city.

Ticking these boxes narrows your list of branches from “all” to a manageable two or three.

vii. Entrance Exams & Admission Process for 2025-2026

  • National tests: JEE (Main/Advanced) for many public institutes.
  • State exams: MHT-CET (Maharashtra), KCET (Karnataka), and others.

University-specific : MIT-WPU

Eligibility

Minimum 50% aggregate in PCM or PCB* or Physics & Mathematics with any Technical Vocational Subject. Physics, Mathematics / Biology* & English are Compulsory subjects with Chemistry/Biotechnology* or Technical Vocational Courses in 10+2/Class 12th or equivalent examination [*Applicable only for Bioengineering programme] (at least 45% marks, in case of Reserved category candidate belonging to Maharashtra state only). OR Minimum 60% marks in Diploma in Engineering & Technology in an appropriate branch from Government approved technical board.

Selection

The selection process for B.Tech Programmes is based on JEE 2025/ MHT-CET 2025/ PERA 2025/ MHT- CET-B* 2025/ NEET* 2025 score and a Statement of Purpose (500 words) written by the student for those candidates who meet the Eligibility criteria. [*Applicable only for Bioengineering programme]

Plan mock tests six months ahead, then fine-tune weak areas two months before the real date.
