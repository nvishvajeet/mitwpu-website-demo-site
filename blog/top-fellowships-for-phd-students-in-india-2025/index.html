Top Fellowships and Grants for Ph.D Scholars in India for 2025

Discover the best PhD fellowships in India for 2025. Get details on PMRF, CSIR-UGC NET, stipends, eligibility, and how to apply for research funding.

Reconstructed from the retained MIT-WPU public capture. View official source

With over 40,000 doctoral degrees awarded annually in India and numbers rising by approximately 15% each year, Ph.D programmes in the country are witnessing unprecedented growth. Behind this expansion lies the critical support structure of fellowships and grants that make advanced research financially viable for aspiring scholars. A well-funded Ph.D. journey allows researchers to focus entirely on their academic pursuits without financial distractions, potentially transforming both their career trajectory and their contribution to knowledge.

This comprehensive guide highlights the most prestigious and accessible PhD fellowship in India, available for 2025, helping prospective doctoral candidates navigate the funding landscape efficiently.

Government-Sponsored Fellowships

Prime Minister's Research Fellowship (PMRF) The PMRF is India's premier Ph.D. fellowship programme, designed to attract talented students to research-oriented careers. The fellowship aims to retain India's best minds within the national research ecosystem while addressing critical developmental challenges through innovative research. Key features include :

  • Eligibility : Students from top technical institutions (IITs, NITs, IISERs, etc.) pursuing research in science and technology fields
  • Stipend : ₹70,000-₹80,000 monthly (with performance-based enhancements)
  • Research Grant : ₹2 lakhs annually for research expenses
  • Application Process : Bi-annual selection (June/December) based on academic credentials and research proposal quality

CSIR-UGC NET Fellowship This cornerstone PhD grants programme supports scholars across scientific disciplines and includes additional contingency grants for books, travel, and research consumables. Key features include :

  • Selection : Through the joint CSIR-UGC National Eligibility Test conducted twice yearly
  • Funding Structure : ₹31,000 monthly for the initial two years (as JRF), increasing to ₹35,000 for the remaining period (as SRF)
  • Coverage : Chemical Sciences, Earth Sciences, Life Sciences, Mathematical Sciences, Physical Sciences, and others
  • Duration : Five years total with performance evaluation at the two-year mark

DST-INSPIRE Fellowship The Innovation in Science Pursuit for Inspired Research (INSPIRE) Fellowship offers a unique entry pathway. This fellowship particularly benefits talented students from institutions without traditional access to elite research funding. Key features include :

  • Application Route : Direct application without entrance examination for first-rank holders in post-graduate programmes
  • Benefits : Monthly stipend of ₹31,000-₹35,000 with ₹20,000 annual research contingency
  • Disciplines : Focused on basic and applied sciences
  • Selection : Merit-based evaluation of academic records and research proposal

Visvesvaraya Ph.D. Scheme Specifically targeted at Electronics, IT, Computer Science and allied fields, this scheme aims to increase the number of Ph.D. degree holders in electronic systems design and manufacturing sectors. Key features include :

  • Industry Relevance : Strong focus on digital technologies with national priority
  • Provisions : Full-time (₹31,500-₹35,000 monthly) and part-time (₹15,750-₹17,500 monthly) tracks available
  • Application Timeline : Annual calls are typically announced in March-April
  • Special Feature : Includes provision for international research visits

Institution-Specific Research Grants

IIT Research Fellowships The Indian Institutes of Technology offer institution-specific Ph.D. fellowships with unique advantages. These fellowships are available across various departments at all IIT campuses, with specific research focus areas varying by institution.

  • Benefits : Stipends comparable to government fellowships plus access to cutting-edge research infrastructure
  • Teaching Assistantship : Opportunities to gain teaching experience with additional compensation
  • Industry Connections : Enhanced industry collaboration possibilities through institutional networks

TIFR Graduate School Programmes The Tata Institute of Fundamental Research provides interdisciplinary opportunities. TIFR's graduate school model combines coursework with intensive research training in fundamental sciences.

  • Fields : Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Computer Science, and Science Education
  • Support : Comprehensive financial support including housing allowances
  • Unique Feature : Direct integration into advanced research groups from entry

IISC Research Associateships The Indian Institute of Science offers specialised laboratory access and funding to research scholars. These associateships are highly competitive but provide unparalleled research resources in the Indian academic landscape.

  • Research Environment : Access to over 40 departments and specialized centres
  • Funding : Competitive stipends with additional project-based funding opportunities
  • Resources : World-class laboratory facilities and research infrastructure

Category-Specific Scholarships

Rajiv Gandhi National Fellowship Designed for SC/ST candidates pursuing doctoral studies, this fellowship aims to increase the representation of marginalised communities in higher education and research. Key features include :

  • Eligibility : Scholars from Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes enrolled in Ph.D programmes
  • Benefits : Monthly stipend equivalent to UGC-NET rates with annual contingency grant
  • Duration : Five years with performance evaluation at regular intervals

Maulana Azad National Fellowship Supporting scholars from minority communities, this fellowship promotes inclusive research environments across disciplines. Key features include :

  • Target Groups : Students from six notified minority communities (Muslims, Christians, Sikhs, Buddhists, Jains, and Parsis)
  • Structure : JRF/SRF model with corresponding stipend rates
  • Selection : Merit-based selection through national-level examinations

Women Scientist Scheme Addressing gender disparities in research, this scheme provides re-entry opportunities for women researchers after career interruptions. Key features include :

  • Focus : Women scientists who have experienced career breaks
  • Components : Three tracks focusing on basic research (WOS-A), technology development (WOS-B), and societal applications (WOS-C)
  • Support : Stipend of ₹30,000-₹55,000 depending on qualifications and track

National Fellowship for OBC Students Supporting Other Backward Class candidates, this fellowship enhances diversity in research environments across disciplines.

  • Eligibility : OBC students enrolled in Ph.D programmes in Indian universities
  • Benefits : Financial support following UGC norms
  • Selection : Merit-based process with reservation quotas

International Collaborative Opportunities

Fulbright-Nehru Doctoral Research Fellowships This prestigious programme enables Indian scholars to conduct research in the United States. The fellowship facilitates international research collaboration while maintaining a connection to Indian doctoral programmes.

  • Duration : Nine months of research at U.S. institutions
  • Support : Round-trip airfare, monthly stipend, health insurance, and settling-in allowance
  • Fields : Wide range of disciplines from environmental science to public health
  • Requirements : Candidates must be registered for Ph.D. programmes in India and have completed preliminary coursework

Commonwealth Split-Site Scholarships Supporting collaborative research between India and Commonwealth countries, this hybrid model allows scholars to gain international exposure while continuing their Indian doctoral studies.

  • Format : 12 months of research in the UK while remaining registered at an Indian institution
  • Benefits : Full funding including travel, living stipend, and research expenses
  • Focus Areas : Priority development themes including science and technology for development

Indo-US Fellowship for Women in STEMM Promoting gender equity in science, technology, engineering, mathematics, and medicine, this programme specifically addresses gender gaps in international research collaboration.

  • Eligibility : Women scholars enrolled in Ph.D programmes in STEMM fields
  • Duration : 3-6 months of research at U.S. institutions
  • Support Package : Comprehensive funding including travel, accommodation, and research expenses

Newton-Bhabha Fund Facilitating UK-India research partnerships, these PhD grants emphasise collaborative research addressing shared global priorities.

  • Structure : Ph.D. placements at UK institutions for Indian doctoral students
  • Fields : Focus on global challenges including climate change, sustainable agriculture, and public health
  • Duration : 3-6 months with full financial support

Application Strategy

Successful Ph.D. fellowship applications require strategic planning. Many successful candidates apply for multiple fellowships simultaneously, increasing their chances of securing funding.

  • Timeline Management : Create a fellowship application calendar, noting that most major fellowships have application windows between September and March.
  • Documentation : Maintain updated academic records, research publications, and identity documents to streamline multiple applications.
  • Research Proposal : Craft adaptable research proposals that clearly articulate significance, methodology, and expected outcomes.
  • Common Mistakes to Avoid : Don't wait until the last minute, neglect institutional endorsements, or submit generic research proposals.

Conclusion

The landscape of PhD fellowship in India for 2025 offers diverse opportunities across disciplines, institutions, and demographic categories. From prestigious government schemes to international collaborations, these funding sources can transform doctoral journeys by providing financial stability and enhanced research opportunities. Prospective scholars should thoroughly research the eligibility criteria and application processes for fellowships aligned with their research interests and background. With strategic planning and compelling applications, Indian researchers can secure the support needed to make meaningful contributions to global knowledge.
