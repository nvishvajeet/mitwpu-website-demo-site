Building a Career in Robotics : Essential Steps for B.Tech Graduates

Robotics is no longer a matter of science fiction; it is an industry of its own and the future of tech. The field is broad and rapidly changing, from self-driving cars to medical robots powered by AI.

Reconstructed from the retained MIT-WPU public capture. View official source

Robotics is no longer a matter of science fiction; it is an industry of its own and the future of tech. The field is broad and rapidly changing, from self-driving cars to medical robots powered by AI. If you are a B. Tech graduate seeking job opportunities in robotics, this is how to proceed.

Why is robotics a great career choice?

The need for robotics professionals worldwide is increasing because of the developing innovation of computer-based intelligence, automation, and machine learning. Robotics has extensive applications in manufacturing, healthcare, defence, and exploration. New processes will be automated, or new talent will be brought in, rapidly expanding robotics as a field. This opens up doors to high-paying and exciting roles.

The Ultimate Guide to Pursuing Careers in Robotics

Explore the World of Robotics Before you start making your way into this domain, you need to understand what robotics really is. The engineering branch that involves robots or machines is called robotics. It has the convergence of disparate domains, like mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, computer science, and AI. With these basic nuggets, you can comprehend the skills and knowledge you need to get throughout your academic journey.

Pick the Right Educational Path Robotics is a field that requires a strong academic bond. It is best to start your journey with a more specialised degree, such as a B.Tech. in Mechanical Engineering (Robotics and Automation). Students get hands-on training in robotics, automation, and AI , as well as a solid foundation in engineering principles.

When pursuing your degree, make sure to take courses in mechatronics, control systems, robotics programming, or artificial intelligence. These skills will help you stand out in the industry.

Develop Technical Skills Here are Essential Technical Skills to Master Robotics

  • Mathematics : data manipulation, matrix calculations, and advanced statistics.
  • Programming : Languages such as Python, C++, and Java to build robot control systems. Physics and math are critical for learning kinetics, kinematics, and dynamics.
  • CAD Software : Tools such as AutoCAD or SolidWorks are used to design a robot.
  • Electronics : Learning about sensors, microcontrollers, circuit design, etc., will give you the skills to implement a working robot.

You will have an advantage in the job market by developing these skills while exploring career opportunities in robotics.

Gain Practical Experience Industry readiness will not be achieved solely through theoretical knowledge. Real work experience in the form of internships, workshops , and projects is a must. Seek internships with robotics companies or industries focused on automation.

In addition, work on projects like robotic arms, autonomous vehicles, or drones to build your practical skills.

Competing in robotics competitions such as RoboCup or IEEE Robotics events can expose you to real-world challenges and improve your CV.

Pursue Advanced Learning A bachelor's degree, such as B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering (Robotics and Automation), is a good beginning. However, pursuing a postgraduate course or obtaining advanced certifications in robotics can significantly advance your career.

MIT-WPU offers B.Tech degree programs that cover machine learning, robotics programming, and AI, which can be a good addition to your education. These courses also keep you updated with the latest trends in the field.

Career Opportunities in Robotics

After your education, you will find numerous careers in robotics. Here are some popular roles :

  • Robotics Engineer : Artificial intelligence in robotics has revolutionised the functionality and design of robots across industries like production, health care, and defence.
  • Automation Engineer : Streamline industrial processes through electronics and AI.
  • AI Specialist : Enhance robotic performance by designing intelligent systems.
  • Mechatronics Engineer : Combine mechanical, electronic, and software systems to design advanced robots.
  • Research Scientist : Work on cutting-edge robotics projects in university or corporate R&D labs
  • Control systems engineer : Design control systems that allow robots to execute complex tasks
  • Robotics Technician : Repair and maintain robots to keep operations running smoothly.
  • Software Engineer : Write algorithms and software for use in robotics applications.

Average Salary in Robotics

The salary of the robotics engineer depends on their experience, specialisation, and location. At entry level, the salary would generally be between ₹4 to ₹6 lakhs per annum in India. For example, a Robotics Engineer's average salary in India is approximately ₹4,90,565 per year, as per PayScale.

As professionals advance experience or specialise in areas such as AI-powered robotics, pay will increase.

Based on Glassdoor, the average total earnings for a Robotics Software Engineer In India are ₹8,45,000 per annum, and the average base pay is ₹7,00,000 per annum. However, these figures can differ greatly according to many variables, such as the business, area, and expertise.

How to Stay Ahead in Robotics

Here are some tips to help you succeed in the competitive field of robotics :

  • Keep Up to Date : Read papers and attend conferences.
  • Create a Portfolio : Display your projects, certifications, and internships in a web portfolio.
  • Network : Participate in robotics-related conferences and engage with professionals in the field on platforms such as LinkedIn.
  • Be a Lifelong Learner : Learn about emerging technologies, including AI, IoT, and machine learning.
