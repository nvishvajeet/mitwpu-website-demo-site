From MBA to PhD in Management, Is the Extra Mile Worth It?

In the continuously changing realm of business education, individuals frequently encounter a pivotal moment after finishing their MBA. Read more here.

Reconstructed from the retained MIT-WPU public capture. View official source

In the continuously changing realm of business education, individuals frequently encounter a pivotal moment after finishing their MBA. While numerous individuals jump directly into the corporate sector, some consider extending their educational path by pursuing a PhD after MBA. This choice isn't merely about enhancing your resume with an additional credential, it's about changing your connection to business knowledge from merely consuming it to actively creating it, and possibly making meaningful contributions to the business world.

Yes, it is possible to pursue a PhD after an MBA and is gaining popularity as a route. An MBA provides a strong basis for doctoral studies in management, offering practical business knowledge that can be crucial in research. The MBA experience frequently aids students in pinpointing particular business sectors that need further exploration, resulting in a more directed and intentional shift to the PhD programme. This programme builds upon the foundational knowledge gained during MBA studies, allowing for in-depth research in areas such as finance, marketing, human resources, or organisational behaviour.

The importance of obtaining a PhD following an MBA primarily hinges on your career objectives and individual ambitions. Here’s what makes it valuable :

Research and Academic Depth : Although an MBA delivers practical business skills, a PhD programme allows for the addition of original research to the discipline. You transition from being a knowledge user to a knowledge producer, acquiring expertise that is rare among business professionals. The programme generally includes training in advanced research methods, statistical analysis, and the creation of theoretical frameworks.

Career Diversification : A PhD provides access to academic paths while preserving corporate options. This dual benefit enables graduates to opt for teaching, research, consulting, or senior corporate roles. Numerous PhD graduates become highly coveted authorities in their specific areas of expertise.

Intellectual Engagement : The PhD experience provides unmatched intellectual excitement, enabling you to investigate business theories and practices more profoundly than what an MBA programme allows. You will conduct a critical review of current literature and create inventive solutions for intricate business challenges. Nevertheless, it is crucial to acknowledge the difficulties :

a) Time Commitment : A PhD generally necessitates 4-6 years of focused study following your MBA, which encompasses coursework, comprehensive exams, and dissertation research.

b) Financial Factors : Although numerous programmes provide stipends and research assistant positions, you may experience a decrease in income while studying versus a full-time corporate position.

c) Research Focus : You will need to cultivate robust research abilities and adjust to a more theoretical method when tackling business issues.

The eligibility criteria for a PhD in Management after MBA involves having,

  • A master's degree (MBA) with minimum marks as specified by the university (typically 55-60%)
  • Qualified entrance exams (such as GRE, GMAT, or country-specific tests)
  • Having a research proposal aligned with the department's focus areas
  • Professional experience (requirements vary by institution)
  • Proficiency in English language (TOEFL/IELTS for international programmes)
  • Qualified national eligibility tests like NET/SET/GATE as applicable

1. Research Programme Selection

  • Identify universities offering PhD programmes aligned with your research interests.
  • Evaluate faculty expertise and research publications.
  • Consider funding opportunities and scholarship availability.
  • Review the institution's research infrastructure and resources.

2. Preparation Phase

  • Develop a compelling research proposal.
  • Prepare for entrance examinations.
  • Gather academic transcripts and letters of recommendation.
  • Build a strong academic CV highlighting research potential.
  • Identify potential research supervisors.

3. Application Process

  • Submit applications to multiple universities.
  • Prepare for interviews with faculty members.
  • Review funding options and fellowship opportunities.
  • Present your research proposal to the selection committee.

The job market for PhD graduates in management is varied and fulfilling :

Academic Positions : Take on the role of a professor or researcher at renowned institutions, influencing the next generation of business leaders while engaging in innovative research. Numerous universities favour PhD graduates for permanent faculty roles.

Corporate Research : Drive research and development efforts within major companies, applying academic rigour to address business challenges. Organisations appreciate PhD graduates for their research and analytical skills.

Consulting : Provide expert consulting services grounded in extensive theoretical knowledge and research proficiency. Numerous PhD graduates create thriving consulting businesses centred around their specific fields of expertise.

Think Tanks and Policy Development : Aid in the creation of policies and strategic planning at governmental or private research organisations. Individuals with PhDs frequently impact industry norms and optimal practices.

Leadership Roles : Assume higher management positions with improved analytical and strategic skills. The blend of practical MBA experience and PhD research expertise renders graduates significant assets in leadership roles.

Pursuing a PhD in Management after MBA requires a significant commitment but offers unique rewards as well. It's not just about adding another degree, but about transforming your relationship with business knowledge and opening doors to diverse career opportunities. The decision to pursue this path must align with your long-term career goals and passion for research and academia. For those who enjoy exploring business concepts, conducting research, and contributing to the academic community, a PhD after MBA can be incredibly fulfilling. Though this journey demands dedication and resilience, the rewards like intellectual growth, career opportunities, and the ability to contribute to business knowledge make it a worthwhile consideration for ambitious MBA graduates. The key is to carefully evaluate your research interests, and career aspirations before embarking on this academic journey. With proper planning and commitment, a PhD in management can be the stepping stone to a rewarding career in academia, research, or high-level business leadership.
