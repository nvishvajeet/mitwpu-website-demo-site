Mahatma Gandhi's message of "Go to the Villages" gradually transformed by the twenty-first century. It came to be interpreted instead as "Go to the Cities"—a shift driven primarily by the rapid pace of urbanisation. Much like it altered many other aspects of life, the twenty-first century also reshaped the character of Indian cities.

These cities—once sprawling haphazardly in every direction—suddenly began to evolve into "smart" entities and acquired a new designation as "smart cities." From that moment, the entire landscape of Civil Engineering began to transform as well. It became Civil Engineering 2.0.

Defining Civil Engineering 2.0

Civil Engineering 2.0 marks the dawn of a new era in urban development and is being applauded as the future of civil engineering. With reference to smart cities, this transition primarily encompasses the extensive application of modern technology in civic development, the creation and efficient management of eco-friendly resources, and the integration of key elements such as sustainability, safety, and security.

The Role of Civil Engineers in Smart Cities

From the very beginning, the role of civil engineers was integral in the development of cities and infrastructural growth. Civil engineers are the experts who design smart cities by considering both the present-day lifestyle needs and future development. Their work involves a variety of different elements. Let us explore some of the most important of these elements in detail.

Transport System: Nowadays, urban transportation has emerged as a major challenge. With the steady growth of the population, existing public transport facilities are gradually proving to be inadequate. Furthermore, the rising number of petrol and diesel-powered vehicles is exacerbating pollution-related issues. In such a scenario, measures such as developing smart traffic control systems, encouraging the production of electric vehicles (developed using modern technology) or establishing a metro network (for public transportation) offer viable solutions.

Water Management: No smart city can exist without effective water management. Civil engineers utilise state-of-the-art technology to establish smart water supply systems that focus not only on providing clean water but also pay special attention to its efficient recycling and usage. They also consider measures to reduce water wastage.

Waste management: In smart cities, data analytics and sensors are used for waste disposal and management, which also encompasses the waste recycling process.

Green Buildings: Green building construction primarily includes elements such as solar power systems, green roofs, eco-friendly construction materials, and energy efficiency. As cities grow, the demand for such buildings is also increasing. Through green buildings, the problem of carbon emissions can also be controlled, leading to a healthy ecosystem for urban living.

Skills required to develop Smart Cities and Green Buildings

Civil engineering students must expand their understanding beyond the traditional civil engineering knowledge and master the following skills to stay relevant.

Building Information Modelling (BIM): BIM is a collaborative digital process for creating data-rich 3D models. This technique allows civil engineers to enhance the efficiency of construction projects via clash detection, costing estimation and more.

Data Analysis: Civil engineering students must learn Data Analytics as a key skill in modern times, in order to analyse data and monitor system performance in smart cities.

Sustainable and Green Building: Civil engineers must also gain a thorough knowledge of green materials, LEED Certification, sustainability, and energy-efficient design.

Career Scope

Breaking the traditional construction patterns and implementing smart ones is proving highly beneficial to the civil engineering sector. With the rise of smart cities and green buildings, many civil engineers are placed with jobs in smart city projects. Moreover, an ample number of career opportunities are emerging in this field. Let us take a look at some of the prominent emerging profiles below.

  • Site Engineer
  • Construction Management Consultant
  • Project Manager
  • Structural Consultant
  • Structural Designer
  • Surveying Consultant
  • GIS Analyst
  • Town Planner
  • Quantity Surveyor

Final thoughts

The face of cities is changing radically due to increasing urbanisation. Hence, it has become all the more important now to develop cities as smart and eco-friendly spaces that are equipped with cutting-edge technology and are based on a sustainability model. It is indeed the need of the hour, and is paving the way to Civil Engineering 2.0 and green building projects as the solutions for problems such as pollution, population growth, resulting environmental damage, and declining urban quality of life. These solutions will indeed be an integral part of the future of civil engineering or the urban way of living, leading to many career opportunities available in this field, and considering the future, adopting Civil Engineering 2.0 from traditional civil engineering will be beneficial for future engineers.
