The MBA Finance curriculum is crafted to equip aspiring finance professionals with the knowledge and skills necessary to excel in today's dynamic business landscape. The programme offers several finance courses covering corporate finance, investment management, financial modeling, risk management, and financial markets. According to their academic journey, students can specialize in their areas of interest to align with their career aspirations. With a solid academic structure that integrates theory with practical applications, the programme prepares graduates for diverse career paths in finance, including roles in corporate finance, investment banking, asset management, and financial consulting.

Business school admissions are highly competitive, as the selection criteria are based on aptitude marks. As graduation, individuals emerge with a prestigious MBA degree and the expertise to navigate complex financial environments, making impactful contributions to the global business landscape.

Core Courses in an MBA Finance Programme

Core courses in an MBA Finance programme provide students with a solid foundation in essential financial principles and practices. These courses cover key areas such as corporate finance, investment management, financial modeling, risk management, financial markets, business analytics, financial statement analysis, valuation, and mergers and acquisitions. Corporate finance courses also cover capital budgeting, financial planning, and capital structure decisions, preparing students to make strategic financial decisions within organizations. Investment management courses focus on portfolio theory, asset allocation, and investment strategies, equipping students with the skills to manage investment portfolios effectively. Financial modeling papers teach students how to construct and analyze financial models to support decision-making processes. Risk management courses cover various types of financial risk, including market, credit, and operational risks, and explore techniques for reducing these risks.

Financial markets courses help students understand financial markets and institutions, while business analytics courses introduce students to data-driven decision-making techniques. Financial statement analysis courses teach students how to interpret and analyze financial statements to assess a company's financial health and performance. Valuation courses focus on methods for valuing assets, businesses, and securities, while mergers and acquisitions courses cover the process and strategies involved in mergers, acquisitions, and corporate restructuring. Overall, core courses in an MBA Finance program help students understand financial theory and practice, preparing them for successful careers in various finance-related roles.

MBA Curriculum at MIT-WPU

The MBA in Finance programme at MIT-WPU is meticulously designed to equip students with a holistic understanding of financial management while concentrating on analytical and problem-solving skills. Through theoretical knowledge and practical insights, students learn financial analytics, corporate finance, financial modeling, and wealth management. The interdisciplinary programme enables students to analyze financial reports, economic trends, and optimize investment portfolios to guide businesses towards sustainable growth and profitability.

Focusing on real-world applications, the syllabus integrates industry-oriented projects, case studies, and interactive learning sessions supported by guest lectures and visits to financial institutions. There's also an internship opportunity where students get hands-on experience where they learn to apply theory to practical solutions. With a focus on promoting critical thinking and ethical decision-making, the MBA Finance curriculum at MIT-WPU empowers students to become successful in the dynamic business landscape.

Admission and Eligibility Criteria

For MBA admissions at MIT-WPU , candidates must have a percentage of 60% in their bachelor’s degree from a UGC-approved university or equivalent (with at least 55% for Maharashtra State reserved category candidates). Candidates must also possess an average percentage in National Level Test such as CAT, XAT, NMAT, GMAT, PERA, CMAT, MAH-CET, or Sep MAT. The selection process will be based on their entrance exam score, performance in group discussions, personal interview, marks in UG, and work experience. This holistic approach ensures that candidates with diverse backgrounds and experiences are considered for admission to the MBA programme to experience a dynamic learning environment.
