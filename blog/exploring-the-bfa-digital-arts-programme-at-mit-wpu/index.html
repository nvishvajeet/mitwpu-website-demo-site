The Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA) in Digital Arts at MIT-WPU is a dynamic programme designed to equip students with cutting-edge digital creativity and artistic expression skills. This degree offers a blend of traditional fine arts training with contemporary digital techniques, preparing students for diverse careers in the digital arts industry. Throughout the programme, students benefit from state-of-the-art design labs and technology-driven learning environments that foster innovation and technical proficiency.

The curriculum has various extra-curricular activities, workshops, and industry collaborations that enhance the learning experience and provide practical exposure. A strong emphasis on placements ensures graduates are well-prepared to secure rewarding positions in animation, game design, graphic design, and multimedia production. With a BFA in Digital Arts, students can become digital artists, ready to contribute significantly to the creative industries.

Understanding the BFA (Digital Arts) Programme and its Curriculum at MIT-WPU

The Bachelor of Fine Arts programme in Digital Arts at MIT-WPU offers an excellent curriculum for developing technical skills and creative expertise. Students engage in foundational courses like Drawing Studio - Live Sketching, Object Drawing - Fundamentals, and Perspective Drawing - Introduction, which build essential skills in visual representation. The programme emphasizes a blend of traditional and digital techniques, with courses such as Material Exploration - Haptic and Advanced Excel, ensuring a well-rounded education. Students also explore critical thinking, environmental sustainability, and social leadership, reflecting MIT-WPU’s commitment to holistic education.

The curriculum is filled with university core courses and electives so students can specialize in game art, concept art, and interactive media. Practical experience is gained through projects and internships, preparing students for animation, game design, graphic design, and multimedia production careers. With a strong focus on placements, MIT-WPU prepares graduates in a way that makes them ready for the dynamic and evolving digital arts industry.

Career Paths and Opportunities after BFA (Digital Arts) Programme

Graduates of the Bachelor of Fine Arts in Digital Arts programme enjoy several career paths and opportunities in the rapidly evolving digital arts industry. They can pursue roles such as digital artists, animators, game designers, and multimedia producers at animation studios, game development companies, advertising agencies, and multimedia firms. They can also excel in graphic, web, and user experience (UX) design, where creative and technical skills are highly valued.

Additionally, graduates can specialize in concept art, character design, and visual effects, contributing to film, television, and interactive media projects. Freelancing and entrepreneurship can also be exciting for those looking to work independently or start their own digital arts businesses.

Scholarships and Admission Criteria

MIT-WPU offers a range of schrships to support outstanding students. The prestigious Dr. Vishwanath Karad Scholarship covers 100% of tuition fees for students achieving 90% or above in the General Category and 85% or above in the Reserved Category. The MIT-WPU Scholarship I provide a 50% tuition fee waiver for those scoring 88% and above (General Category) and 83% and above (Reserved Category). The MIT-WPU Scholarship II offers a 25% fee waiver for students with scores of 85% and above (General Category) and 81% and above (Reserved Category).

To be eligible for admission, candidates must have a minimum score of 50% in their 10+2/Class 12th or equivalent examination with English as a subject and 45% for Reserved Class category candidates from Maharashtra.

The selection process comprises three key stages: a Drawing Test, a Portfolio Assessment, and a Personal Interaction with a Jury for a comprehensive evaluation. Students with desired scores in the UCEED/PERA/MAH CET (Applied Arts) Examination will be exempted from the Drawing Test and directly proceed to the Portfolio Assessment and Personal Interaction rounds.
