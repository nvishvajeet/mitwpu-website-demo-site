Your Guide to MBA Entrance Exams and Tips to Prepare

This guide will walk you through the the most popular MBA entrance exams in India and tips to prepare for them, interspersed with the best MBA programmes at MIT-WPU which might just be your gateway to success.

So, you’ve made up your mind to pursue an MBA. That’s a great step! But if the slew of entrance exams - CAT, MAT, XAT and so on - has you feeling overwhelmed. You’re not the only one.

Selecting the correct examination to take, along with doing the necessary preparations, is essential to get an entry into a prestigious MBA programme.

This guide will walk you through the the most popular MBA entrance exams in India and tips to prepare for them, interspersed with the best MBA programmes at MIT-WPU which might just be your gateway to success.

Why Are MBA Entrance Exams Important?

MBA entrance exams are not just a mere formality. They evaluate critical thinking, problem-solving, and communication skills - all of which are essential in business. These tests are the gateway to entering the reputed B-schools which can shape your career, have the right network and create high-paying job opportunities.

We will discuss the essential exams you can go for, their structure, and how to ace these exams.

What is CAT? IIMs conduct CAT, the most popular MBA entrance exam in India. They accept it for their consideration, as top B-schools across the government.

Eligibility :

  • A bachelor's degree scoring a minimum of 50% marks (45% for SC/ST/PwD aspirants).
  • You can also apply if you are in your final year.

Exam Pattern : The exam is divided into the following sections

  • Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension
  • Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning
  • Quantitative Ability.

Time : 2 hours (40 minutes per each section mentioned above)

Mode : Computer-based.

Application Process : You can register in August and the exam is in November. Register for CAT on the official website.

Tips to Prepare : The CAT is a highly competitive exam, and hence preparation is indispensable.

  • Read lots of content to boost reading and comprehension skills.
  • Focus on time management skills through mock tests.
  • Practice regularly if you are weaker in subjects like the Quantitative Ability.

CAT, conducted by the IIMs, is the most sought-after MBA entrance exam in India. It’s widely accepted by top B-schools across the country.

What is MAT? MAT, which is administered by the AIMA, is also known for its flexibility and is conducted four times a year. It is accepted at more than 600 colleges across India.

  • Graduation in any discipline.
  • Applications can be made by final-year students.

Exam Pattern : The exam is divided into the following sections - Language Comprehension, Mathematical Skills, Data Analysis, Intelligence & Critical Reasoning and General Awareness.

Duration : 150 questions in 120 minutes.

Mode of examination : Paper-based, computer-based/ internet-based test

Application Process : To apply, visit the AIMA MAT website.

Tips to Prepare :

  • Develop a solid base for logical and data interpretation.
  • Solve questions based on speed.
  • Read more newspapers and magazines for general awareness.

What is XAT? XLRI Jamshedpur conducts the XAT, which has a distinctive Decision-Making section, making it one of the toughest MBA entrance tests.

  • A bachelor’s degree, regardless of field.
  • Students in their final year can apply.

Exam Pattern :

The exam is divided into the following sections - Verbal & Logical Ability, Decision-Making, Quantitative Ability & Data Interpretation, and General Knowledge.

Duration : 3.30 hours.

Application Process : Registration starts from July to December and the actual exam happens in January (once a year).

Through the XAT official website.

Tips to Prepare : Another important area in XAT is Decision making.

  • Practice case studies and mock tests regularly.
  • Prepare for GRE Reading and Mathematics.

What is CMAT? CMAT is a simple test, accepted by all institutions recognised by the AICTE, and is conducted by the NTA.

  • A bachelor’s degree in any field.
  • Students in their final year may apply.

Exam Pattern : The exam is divided into the following sections - Quantitative Techniques, Logical Reasoning, Language Comprehension, General Awareness, and Innovation & Entrepreneurship.

Duration : 3 hours.

Application Process : Register through the NTA CMAT site.

Tips to Prepare : Make sure to revise your fundamentals in Quantitative techniques and Logical skills. Solve previous year's question papers to understand the pattern and speed needed to tackle the questions.

Reasons for Applying for MBA at MIT-WPU

An MBA is a great step ahead in your career, spend enough time finding the right university, it really matters. Among the top institutes in India, MIT World Peace University (MIT-WPU) is different because it takes a holistic approach. The curriculum of its MBA programmes aims at shaping future-ready leaders and integrates elements of academic excellence with practical application.

In fact, MIT-WPU offers several MBA specialisations such as Finance, HR, Marketing, Operations, and more (see their programmes here ). These programmes are designed to the requirements of the modern competitive business environment.

The faculty consists of a mix of industry professionals and academic scholars. The curriculum goes beyond theory, featuring case studies, simulations, and capstone projects that set you up to face real-world challenges.

At MIT-WPU, you’ll benefit from robust industry tie-ups leading to internship and placement opportunities galore. Connecting these dots between academia and the field of practice is useful for everyone involved.

As part of its international exposure, the university has international immersion programmes for students to learn business management with a global approach.

MBA Eligibility for MIT-WPU

To seek admission to an MBA at MIT-WPU, you should fulfil the following eligibility criteria :

  • Graduation with at least 60% aggregate (55% for the Reserved category of the Maharashtra State.
  • Class 10 and Class 12– overall at least 60% aggregate marks (English as subjects)

A valid non-zero score in one or more of the following exams: CAT, XAT, NMAT, GMAT, CMAT, MAH-CET or MAT.

An entrance exam score, Group Discussion (GD), Personal Interaction (PI), previous academic records, Statement of Purpose (SOP), and work experience are all considered in the selection process.
