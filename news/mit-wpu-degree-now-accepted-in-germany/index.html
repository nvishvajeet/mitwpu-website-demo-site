We are proud to announce that MIT World Peace University (MIT-WPU) is now officially recognised in Germany, with an H+ rating in the Anabin database. Anabin is the German government's authoritative database that offers comprehensive information on the accreditation and acceptance of foreign higher education degrees. With the prestigious H+ rating, MIT-WPU is acknowledged as equivalent to a German Hochschule (Institute of Higher Education), marking a significant achievement for our institution.

Check here - https://anabin.kmk.org/no_cache/filter/institutionen.html What Does H+ Mean?

The H+ rating in the Anabin database signifies that MIT-WPU’s degrees are fully recognised by German authorities. This recognition is crucial for students and graduates who wish to:

Pursue further studies in Germany: MIT-WPU graduates can now seamlessly apply for higher education programmes in German universities. Seek employment in Germany: Employers in Germany will recognise MIT-WPU degrees as equivalent to those granted by German institutions, enhancing job prospects.

Validate degrees: The H+ rating acts as the binding reference for German administrations, employers, and private individuals to verify the legitimacy and equivalency of foreign degrees.

Why is This Important?

Being listed in the Anabin database with an H+ rating is a milestone that reinforces MIT-WPU's commitment to academic excellence on a global scale. This recognition opens doors for our graduates to explore educational and professional opportunities in one of the world's leading economies. We are excited to continue expanding the global reach of MIT-WPU degrees and empowering our students with world-class education that transcends borders.
