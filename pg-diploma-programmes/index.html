Your Path to Tech-Enabled Global Leadership Begins Here

The Postgraduate Diploma (PGD) in Management is a one-year, full-time programme for graduates seeking a strong, accelerated grounding in management and leadership. It blends core managerial learning with leadership development to support rapid career growth or a global academic pathway.

The programme is strengthened by structured academic practices such as Collaborative Academic Dialogues (CAD), Curriculum Alignment & Teaching Strategy (CATS) and defined learning goals, ensuring clarity, quality and industry relevance. Student-faculty platforms like Open House and Synergy Circle foster transparency and engagement, while initiatives like Future Fridays keep the academic environment dynamic and forward-focused.

A Globally Benchmarked, Immersive Experiential Academic Framework

The programme follows a globally benchmarked academic architecture that integrates strong management foundations, leadership development, applied learning, and global exposure. The curriculum is designed for progressive capability building, moving from core management principles to strategic integration and real-world application.

Learning is anchored in a case-based and experiential framework, drawing from Harvard Business School, Ivey Business School, and ISB case repositories, and reinforced through simulations, role plays, ERP-based platforms, and applied analytics tools, ensuring a seamless bridge between theory and managerial practice.

Artificial Intelligence is embedded across the curriculum to enhance decision-making, problem-solving, and leadership effectiveness, keeping the programme aligned with contemporary and future business environments.

The academic journey is structured across two intensive semesters, each with a clear focus and learning objective.

Semester I: Foundations of Management and Leadership

Semester I builds a strong grounding in core management disciplines and leadership fundamentals. The focus is on developing analytical thinking, managerial competence, and leadership awareness.

Key elements of Semester I include:

  • Core management foundations across functional areas
  • Leadership Lab, running alongside academic courses to develop self-awareness, communication, and leadership behaviours
  • Exposure to AI-enabled business learning and data-driven decision-making
  • Preparation and academic alignment for the Global Immersion Programme
  • Early integration of case-based learning, simulations, and collaborative problem-solving

This semester establishes the intellectual and behavioural foundation required for advanced strategic learning.

Semester II: Strategic Integration and Application

Semester II emphasises strategic management, specialisation depth, and real-world application. Students move from understanding business functions to integrating them at an organisational and strategic level.

Key elements of Semester II include:

  • Advanced courses in strategy, corporate finance, and specialisation electives
  • Continued Leadership Lab, focusing on decision-making, influence, and execution
  • Applied learning through industry-linked assignments and projects
  • Internship-based learning, enabling students to work on real organisational challenges
  • Completion of the Industry-Integrated Capstone Research Project, synthesising learning from across the programme

This semester is designed to sharpen strategic thinking, industry readiness, and professional confidence.

The programme culminates in an Industry-Integrated Capstone Research Project, where students apply concepts, frameworks, and analytical tools to address real business problems. The capstone encourages:

  • Cross-functional integration of management knowledge
  • Evidence-based decision-making
  • Industry relevance and practical impact

Internship-based learning complements the capstone, offering students direct exposure to organisational contexts and managerial challenges.

Learning Outcomes

Through this structure, students develop:

  • Strong functional and strategic management capabilities
  • Leadership competence supported by continuous lab-based development
  • Ability to apply analytics, AI tools, and ERP systems in managerial contexts
  • Experience in handling real-world business problems through projects and research
  • A global and ethical perspective on leadership and decision-making

Impactful Case-Based Pedagogy

The programme follows a modular, case-based, and experiential pedagogy, designed to promote active learning and practical application. Teaching and learning are driven through classroom discussions, simulations, role plays, cross-functional teamwork, and reflective practice, enabling students to apply concepts to real managerial contexts.

Learning is supported through a hybrid-enabled model, combining intensive on-campus engagement with curated global digital resources, ensuring depth, flexibility, and continuity in the learning experience.

Leadership & Technology Integration

Leadership development is continuous through psychometric assessments, mentoring, and values-based reflection. Students gain exposure to modern-day tools and platforms, including Python, R, Tableau, Power BI, SAP, and ERP systems, with AI integrated into learning, decision-making, and career planning.

Students are encouraged to build self-awareness, ethical judgment, strategic thinking, and decision-making capability alongside academic learning.

Technology-enabled learning supports leadership effectiveness and managerial decision-making, without compromising the programme’s full-time, immersive, on-campus learning experience.

Programme Outcomes

Graduates emerge as globally competent, industry-ready, and ethically grounded business leaders, enabling them to:

  • Apply strong functional management knowledge across key business domains.
  • Demonstrate effective leadership, decision-making, and ethical judgment.
  • Solve complex business problems using consulting-oriented and analytical approaches.
  • Integrate strategy, finance, operations, and people management in real-world contexts.
  • Operate confidently in global and multicultural business environments.
  • Leverage industry exposure and networks for accelerated career progression.
  • Engage in lifelong learning and continued executive development pathways.

Admissions

Eligibility

Minimum 60% aggregate marks in 3/ 4-year graduation degree in any stream from a UGC-approved institution/ university or its equivalent (Minimum 55% aggregate marks for candidates belonging to the Reserved Category from Maharashtra State).

AND

A minimum of 60% aggregate marks in Class 10 (SSC) and Class 12 (HSC) examinations, with English as a compulsory subject.

Selection Process

Admission will be based on the Group Discussion (GD), Personal Interaction (PI) score conducted by MIT-WPU as per the prescribed schedule, along with consideration of their Past Academic Record and Work Experience.
