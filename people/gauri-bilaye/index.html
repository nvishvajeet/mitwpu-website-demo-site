About

My philosophy as a teacher will be based on stimulating curiosity, critical thinking, and problem solving in the real world. My goal is to build a non-discriminatory, interactive learning classroom, in which students form an active group, challenge their thoughts and possibilities, and relate theory with practice. As a teacher, my driving power is to guide students towards achieving not only academic results by also teaching them how to achieve ethical values, creativity and life-long learning. In line with the vision of the holistic and value-based education at MIT-WPU, I would be able to make a contribution through creative teaching methods, interdisciplinary cooperation, and the learning process based on research that would help students become responsible professional beings and influential global citizens.
