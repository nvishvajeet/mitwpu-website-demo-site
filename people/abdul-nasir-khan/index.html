Dr. Abdul Nasir Khan

Assistant Professor

About

Dr. Abdul Nasir Khan is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at MIT World Peace University. He completed his M.Sc. in Statistics in 2015 from Aligarh Muslim University, Aligarh, and subsequently pursued his PhD at the same university, which he completed in 2022. After completing his PhD, Dr. Khan joined the Department of Community Medicine at Maharishi Markandeshwar Medical College & Hospital in Solan, Himachal Pradesh, as an Assistant Professor, where he served from March 2022 to October 2022. He then joined MIT World Peace University as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics. Dr. Khan's research interests include Order Statistics, Records Values, Generalized Order Statistics, and Statistical Inference. He has published 7 research papers in reputed international journals and has presented his research at several national and international conferences in India. Dr. Khan's expertise in the field of Statistics and his research contributions make him a valuable member of the academic community. He is committed to advancing the field of Statistics and to teaching and mentoring the next generation of statisticians.

Research interests

Ordered random variables, Bayesian inference, parameter estimation, and biostatistics.
