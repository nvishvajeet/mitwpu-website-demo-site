Dr. Prashant Mahadeo Patane

Assistant Professor

About

Prof. Prashant Patane is an accomplished academician and mechanical engineer, currently serving as an Assistant Professor at Dr. Vishwanath Karad MIT World Peace University, Pune. With a solid background in Thermal Engineering and extensive experience in Engine Simulation software such as GT-Power, Matlab-Simulink & CFD, he plays a vital role in shaping the minds of the future generation of engineers. He completed his M.Tech in Thermal Engg. with CGPA 8.11 from the College of Engg. Pune, and is currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from the College of Engineering, Pune. Prior to his academic career, he worked with SKF India Ltd, Pune.

Research interests

BTMS, CFD, IC Engines, Combustion, Alternate Fuels

Publications

  1. Laser-Induced Spark Ignition of Methane-Air Mixtures in Constant Volume Combustion Chamber
  2. Battery Thermal Management System for EVs: A Review
  3. CFD Simulation of Gaseous Fuel Combustion in Constant Volume Combustion Chamber using Multi-Point Laser Ignition
  4. A study on impact of various solar panel cleaning methods on its performance
  5. Numerical simulation of combustion characteristics and emission predictions of methane-air and hydrogen-air mixtures in a constant volume combustion chamber using multi-point Laser Ignition
  6. Multipoint laser ignition system and its applications to IC engines
