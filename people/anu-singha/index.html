About

Dr. Anu Singha is a seasoned academician and researcher, currently working as an Assistant Professor in the School of Computer Science & Engineering at Dr. Vishwanath Karad MIT World Peace University (MIT-WPU), Pune. He obtained his Ph. D. in Computer Science and Engineering from Tripura Central University in 2021, following his M. Tech. completion in 2015. His educational background also includes an MCA from South Asian University, New Delhi. He initiated his research career as a Junior/Senior Research Fellow (JRF/SRF) under a DRDO-sponsored project at the Computer Vision Laboratory, Tripura University. Dr. Singha’s research expertise lies in artificial intelligence, with a strong focus on machine learning and deep learning techniques, particularly convolutional neural networks (CNNs). His work includes object detection in adverse weather conditions and several impactful healthcare applications, such as histopathology-based breast cancer detection, MRI-based brain tumor classification, knee joint inflammation detection, and fetus detection using medical imaging. His broader research interests cover computer vision, image and medical image processing, human–computer interaction, and text analytics. He has an extensive publication record in reputed SCI Indexed journals/IEEE Transactions and top Tier conferences, along with multiple granted and published patents. He has actively contributed as a reviewer, chaired technical sessions at conferences, and delivered expert lectures in faculty development programs (FDPs) and workshops.
