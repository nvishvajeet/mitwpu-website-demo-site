Dr. Nithya N Kutty

Assistant Professor

About

Dr. Nithya N. Kutty has completed her doctoral study from IIT Kharagpur. Her doctoral study was focused on dynamics of scent volatiles synthesis, storage and emission in flowers of tuberose, a widely cultivated ornamental plant in Asian countries. During her doctoral study (through CSIR JRF/SRF fellowship), she has also carried out collaborative research work in the area of metabolite profiling from plant endophytes, hairy roots of carrots and studied the effect of gamma rays on floral volatiles. She has also presented her research work in several national and international conferences. She pursued her post-graduation (M Tech) in Agricultural biotechnology from IIT Kharagpur and completed her graduation (B Tech Biotechnology) from JNTU Hyderabad. Her research area of interests includes plant specialized metabolism, plant-microbe interactions, plant volatiles and metabolomics. Presently she is teaching basic and applied courses at undergraduate, postgraduate and doctoral levels. She is a recognized PhD supervisor of the university. She also mentors students for their research project. Recently, she could secure "DST Young scientist and Technologist" grant under which she is working on vaporization of waste marigold flowers using natural deep eutectic solvents. Dr. Kutty could also complete EMBO lab leadership course with full support from EMBO. She also holds two published Indian patents and one granted patent through her research in the domain of green chemistry.

Research interests

Plant specialised metabolism, plant–microbe interactions, plant volatiles, metabolomics, and green chemistry.
