Advanced Materials & Energy Systems Research

Discover advanced materials and manufacturing research at WPU Goa focused on energy systems, innovation, and next-generation technologies.

Role context
Names and roles remain in their institute, centre or advisory context. Membership is not treated as a faculty appointment.

Rationale

  • Growing demand for advanced materials in aerospace, transportation, electronics, sensing, and healthcare, as well as in strategic sectors such as agriculture, defence, aerospace industries.
  • Need for sustainable, affordable, green, and energy-efficient materials manufacturing processes.
  • India’s strategic focus on strengthening materials manufacturing capabilities under initiatives like Make in India and Atmanirbhar Bharat.
  • Opportunity to position WPU GŌA as an international leader in advanced materials research. Innovation, IP creation, translation to industrial sector.
  • Bridging the gap between academic research and industrial applications in materials research.

Objectives

  • Advance transdisciplinary research in advanced materials, manufacturing and emergent device systems.
  • Design and develop new materials and affordable green processing methodologies for multiple industrial and societal applications.
  • Foster industry-academia-policy convergence for translational impact.
  • Train next-generation researchers and engineers through experimential learning and hands-on training.
  • Build global partnerships to strengthen India’s role in advanced manufacturing and emergent device development ecosystems.

Mandate

The Institute will advance research and innovation in advanced materials and manufacturing technologies as well as emergent device systems in multiple sectors of national importance and international significance such as semiconductors, quantum, advanced sensors, robotics, AI/ML etc. By integrating science, engineering, and industry collaboration. Its mandate is to develop sustainable materials, green, smart and energy efficient manufacturing, and translational research leading to IP creation (patents), high impact publications, and proof-of-concept demonstrations (TRL 4) that could invite industry interest.

  • Opportunity to position MIT-WPU Goa as an international leader in advanced materials research. Innovation, IP creation, translation to industrial sector.

Impact and Outcomes

  • Development of new materials, affordable and green manufacturing technologies as well as new architectures of emergent devices for energy, environment, transportation, robotics and other such sectors.addressing, industrial and societal challenges.
  • Creation of startups and patents driving India’s manufacturing competitiveness.
  • Enhanced student employability and global competitiveness.
  • Strengthened academic-industry-policy linkages.
  • Contribution to Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

Partnerships and Alliances

  • Collaborations with international universities and research centres in materials and manufacturing.
  • Alliances with industry leaders in aerospace, automotive, electronics, healthcare and strategic sectors such as defence, agriculture, aerospace, water etc.
  • Engagement with government agencies and policy think-tanks.
  • Partnerships with NGOs and community organizations for societal outreach.

Infrastructure and Resources

  • State-of-the-art laboratories for materials synthesis, characterization, and manufacturing.
  • Computational facilities for materials modelling and simulation.
  • Innovation hubs and incubator spacesfor startups.
  • Collaborative classrooms and seminar halls.
  • Access to shared resources across MIT-WPU campuses.

Members

Prof. Neil Robertson

Professor, School of Chemistry, The University of Edinburgh Prof. Neil Robertson is a Professor in the School of Chemistry at the University of Edinburgh, where he leads the Robertson Research Group. He graduated with a BSc and PhD from the University of Edinburgh and carried out post-doctoral research at the Freie Universität Berlin and the University of Wales, Bangor. His research focuses on the synthesis, characterisation, and application of new materials for energy conversion and storage, including lithium-ion, lithium-sulphur, sodium-ion, and zinc-ion battery electrode materials, supercapacitor and pseudocapacitor electrodes, and dye-sensitised and perovskite solar cells. He currently serves as Dean of International Partnerships for the College of Science & Engineering at the University of Edinburgh, and as President Elect of the Materials Chemistry Community of the Royal Society of Chemistry. He has collaborated with several industrial partners on battery and solar energy technologies.

Prof. Ashutosh Sharma

Institute Chair Professor, Department of Chemical Engineering, IIT Kanpur Prof. Ashutosh Sharma is an Institute Chair Professor in the Department of Chemical Engineering at IIT Kanpur, and elected President of the Indian National Science Academy (2023–25). He served as Secretary to the Government of India, Department of Science and Technology, from 2015 to 2021, where he initiated landmark programs in quantum technologies, clean energy, innovation, and international scientific collaborations. He has published over 350 peer-reviewed papers with over 20,000 citations, filed 15 patents, and is the founding coordinator of the Nanosciences Centre and Advanced Imaging Centre at IIT Kanpur. His numerous honours include the Padma Shri, the inaugural Infosys Prize in Engineering and Computer Science, the TWAS Science Prize, the Shanti Swarup Bhatnagar Prize, and the UNESCO Medal for Contribution to Development of Nanoscience and Nanotechnology. He is a Fellow of the Indian National Science Academy, Indian Academy of Sciences, and The World Academy of Sciences (TWAS).

Prof. Dipankar Das Sarma

Honorary Professor, Solid State and Structural Chemistry Unit (SSCU), IISc Bangalore Prof. Dipankar Das Sarma (popularly known as D.D. Sarma) is an Honorary Professor at the Solid State and Structural Chemistry Unit (SSCU), IISc Bangalore, where he spent over three decades as a faculty member. He obtained his Integrated MSc in Physics from IIT Kanpur (1977) and his PhD from IISc Bangalore (1982), and is a world leader in three distinct research areas: strongly correlated electron systems, semiconductor nanoparticles, and energy materials. His honours include the Shanti Swarup Bhatnagar Prize (1994), TWAS Prize in Physics (2007), the UNESCO Biennial Javed Husain Prize, and the Italian Government's Knight of "The Order of the Star of Italy" (2014). He has garnered over 22,900 citations with an h-index of 78, and serves as Senior Editor of ACS Energy Letters. He is a Fellow of all four major Indian science academies.
