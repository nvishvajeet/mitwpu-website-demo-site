Research Methodologies

Research That Listens, Learns, and Transforms

In this institution, we work together with a variety of stakeholders to understand the impact of- and ways to address responsible integration of AI in human life. Through transdisciplinary methods, stakeholders are brought together in dialogue to talk about challenges and opportunities for sustainable solutions in various fields, including health, mental wellbeing, education, farming, or social justice.

  • Climate science and data analytics including downscaled projections and impact modelling
  • Transdisciplinary collaboration across engineering, ecology, economics, and social science
  • Policy and governance research supporting evidence-based regulation and institutional reform
  • Pilot projects and adaptive management testing and refining interventions in real-world contexts
  • Community-engaged research ensuring strategies address lived experience and local priorities
  • Nature-based and technological solutions that work synergistically rather than in isolation
  • Lifecycle and systems analysis examining trade-offs and co-benefits across environmental, social, and economic dimensions

Members

Prof. Aditya D. Mohite

Professor, Department of Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering, and Department of Materials Science & Nano Engineering, Rice University Prof. Aditya D. Mohite is a Professor in the Departments of Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering and Materials Science & NanoEngineering at Rice University, Houston, and Director of REINVENTS (Rice Engineering INitiatiVe for ENergy Transition and Sustainability). He earned his B.S. and M.S. in physics and solid-state physics from Maharaja Sayajirao University of Baroda, India, and his PhD in Electrical Engineering from the University of Louisville, before serving as a Staff Scientist at Los Alamos National Laboratory. His research focuses on understanding structure-function properties in materials to control charge and energy transport across interfaces, with applications in next-generation photovoltaics, photocatalysis, and decarbonisation technologies. He has published over 180 peer-reviewed papers in journals including Science, Nature, and Nature Materials, garnering over 25,000 citations, and has been named to the Clarivate Highly Cited Researchers list five consecutive years from 2019 to 2023.

Prof. Amartya Mukhopadhyay

Professor, Department of Metallurgical Engineering & Materials Science, IIT Bombay Prof. Amartya Mukhopadhyay is a Professor in the Department of Metallurgical Engineering & Materials Science at IIT Bombay, and a globally recognised pioneer in sodium-ion (Na-ion) battery technology. He holds a B.E. in Metallurgical Engineering from NIT Durgapur, an M.Tech from IIT Kanpur, and a DPhil in Materials from the University of Oxford, UK, followed by post-doctoral research at Brown University, USA. He was invited by the journal Science to contribute a perspective article in his area of research, published as Li metal battery, heal thyself (Science, 2018). In December 2024, he was honoured with the Tata Transformation Prize in Sustainability, selected from 169 entries across 18 Indian states, for his groundbreaking work in developing safe, affordable, and sustainable Na-ion batteries. He is also a recipient of the SwarnaJayanti Fellowship 2020–21 and the INAE Young Engineer Award 2016.

Prof. Pradeep Thalappil

Institute Professor, Department of Chemistry, IIT Madras Prof. Pradeep Thalappil is an Institute Professor and Deepak Parekh Institute Chair Professor in the Department of Chemistry at IIT Madras, the highest academic distinction at the institute. He earned his PhD in chemical physics from the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, under Professors C.N.R. Rao and M.S. Hegde, followed by post-doctoral fellowships at UC Berkeley and Purdue University. He is an author of over 550 scientific papers and an inventor of over 100 patents, and has co-founded seven companies translating nanotechnology into affordable water purification solutions that have reached over 10 million people. He was elected a Foreign Member of the US National Academy of Engineering in 2024 for his contributions to cluster chemistry and affordable drinking water solutions. His accolades include the Padma Shri, Shanti Swarup Bhatnagar Prize, ENI Award, and VinFuture Prize.
