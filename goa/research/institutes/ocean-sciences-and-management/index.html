Research Methodologies

Research That Listens, Learns, and Transforms

  • We conducting systematic studies of ocean species, ecosystems, and evolving ocean conditions.
  • Developing and deploying advanced instruments, sensors, and AI-enabled systems to support ocean research.
  • Assessing how coastal communities derive, share, and benefit from ocean-based livelihoods and resources.
  • Evaluating legal frameworks and institutional structures to strengthen ocean governance and management.
  • Collaborating with local and Indigenous communities to integrate their knowledge, priorities, and practices.
  • Drawing expertise from science, engineering, economics, law, and cultural studies to address complex ocean challenges..
  • We Implementing and assessing innovative approaches through real-world ocean trials and demonstration sites.

Members

Prof. Gyana Ranjan Tripathy

Associate Professor, Department of Earth and Climate Science, IISER Pune Prof. Gyana Ranjan Tripathy is an Associate Professor in the Department of Earth and Climate Science at IISER Pune, where he leads the GRASP (Geochemical Research for Aquatic and Sedimentary Processes) laboratory. His group primarily aims to quantify low-temperature aquatic processes, including chemical weathering and climate interactions in the Himalayan river system, marine sediment geochemistry, and the global carbon cycle. His research expertise spans weathering, geochronology, and isotope geochemistry. He was selected as a Young Associate of the Indian Academy of Sciences in 2015, recognised for his work on surficial weathering processes, Re-Os geochronology, reconstruction of marine redox conditions of the geological past, and chemical weathering of Indian river basins. He has published extensively in leading international journals including Chemical Geology and Geochimica et Cosmochimica Acta.

Prof. Sunil Kumar Singh

Director, CSIR - National Institute of Oceanography, Goa Prof. Sunil Kumar Singh is the Director of CSIR – National Institute of Oceanography (CSIR-NIO), Goa, and a Professor at the Physical Research Laboratory, Ahmedabad. Born in 1971, he completed his B.Sc. and M.Sc. (Tech.) from Banaras Hindu University and his PhD in Geology from the Physical Research Laboratory and M.S. University, Vadodara. He is a leading Indian geochemist known for his studies in low-temperature elemental and isotope geochemistry, with research that has advanced understanding of the evolution of the Himalayas and biogeochemical cycling in the Indian Ocean. His research interests span geochemistry, isotope chemistry, palaeoclimate, palaeoceanography, nutrient cycling, and biogeochemistry. He has been conferred the Lifetime Achievement Award of the Indian Society of Applied Geochemists (ISAG) for his contributions to Earth Sciences and applied geochemistry.
