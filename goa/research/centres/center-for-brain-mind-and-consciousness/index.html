Centre for Brain, Mind and Consciousness

Explore the Center for Brain, Mind and Consciousness at WPU Goa, advancing research in cognition, human awareness, and interdisciplinary neuroscience.

Prof. Dr. Vishwanath Karad

Rationale

  • Pursuit of academic research and study of Brain, Mind and Consciousness at a graduate and post graduate level
  • Run a “Self-Actualisation Programme” for all students to support holistic emotional, spiritual, and psychological development alongside academic excellence.

Objectives

Aims and Objectives

  • To conduct graduate degree programmes for undergraduate and postgraduate students in Brain, Mind and Consciousness studies
  • To provide instructional programmes for 2 credits per academic year, mandatory for all students under the 'Self Actualization Programme'

Activities

  • Research
  • Training of Staff and Students
  • Mentoring students
  • Counselling

Mandate

  • Deliver education rooted in the rich traditions and legacy of Indian culture, with equal emphasis on values, discipline, compassion, tolerance, and holistic personality development alongside academic excellence.
  • Provide a platform for research in the domains of mind, consciousness, and neuroscience, informed by insights from Indian Knowledge Systems.

Partnerships and Alliances

  • Collaborate with industry and corporate partners to deliver workshops and seminars on team building and soft skills as part of organisational HR initiatives.
  • Maintain liaison with government programmes and policy bodies to promote holistic student development across all levels.

Operational Plan

  • Organise guest lectures by eminent researchers and scholars in the domains of consciousness studies and cognitive science.
  • Conduct annual conferences on themes related to cognitive science, mind, and consciousness.
  • Offer fellowships and internships for international university students.
  • Provide scholarships to deserving students to pursue research at leading higher education institutions.

Outcomes and Impact

  • The centre aims to develop well-rounded individuals and responsible citizens who will serve as ambassadors of the institution, while also becoming specialists in their chosen fields of study.
  • The centre also seeks to provide the perfect platform that fosters the integration of science and spirituality, in its endeavour to pursue research in the largely unexplored domains of consciousness and the chemistry of the mind.

Dr. Deepak Ranade

Director & CEO, Spinexpertz; Consultant Neurosurgeon, YCM Hospital and Institute of Post Graduation Dr. Deepak Ranade is a distinguished Consultant Neurosurgeon and the Director & CEO of Spinexpertz, Pune. He completed his M.Ch. in Neurosurgery from K.E.M. Hospital, University of Bombay in 1994, topping the university with a gold medal, and holds an M.S. in General Surgery and M.B.B.S. from B.J. Medical College, Pune. With a career spanning over 25 years, he has performed more than 2,000 spine surgeries covering the entire spectrum of spinal care, including endoscopic procedures, instrumentation, spinal cord tumors, and cervical spine fixation. He served as Professor and Head of the Department of Neurosurgery at D.Y. Patil Medical College, Pimpri, and remains an Honorary Consultant Neurosurgeon at YCM Hospital. He is also a PhD researcher exploring the study of consciousness in the brain using fMRI.

Dr. Vijay P. Bhatkar

Padma Bhushan; Former Chancellor, Nalanda University Dr. Vijay P. Bhatkar is an internationally acclaimed Indian computer scientist, IT leader, and educationalist. He is best known as the architect of India's National Initiative in Supercomputing, having led the development of the PARAM supercomputers, and as the founder and Executive Director of C-DAC. He served as Chancellor of Nalanda University since January 2017 and previously as Chairman of the Board of Governors of IIT Delhi from 2012 to 2017. He was honoured with the Padma Shri in 2000 and the Padma Bhushan in 2015, along with the Maharashtra Bhushan. A Fellow of IEEE, ACM, and INAE, he has authored 12 books and 80 research papers, with current interests spanning Exascale Supercomputing, AI, and the Synthesis of Science and Spirituality.

Dr. Padma Shastry

Scientist ‘G’, BRIC - National Centre for Cell Science, Pune Dr. Padma Shastry is a distinguished Scientist 'G' at BRIC – National Centre for Cell Science (NCCS), Pune, one of India's premier biotechnology research institutions. She began her career at the Immunology Department of CVTC, KEM Hospital, Parel, Mumbai in 1984, working in the area of immunology of acute rheumatic fever and rheumatic heart disease, and has been associated with NCCS since 1990. She developed an in vitro system using cell lines as alternatives to animals for assessing antibodies to tetanus. Her research interests lie in cancer biology, with current projects focused on studying drug resistance and sensitivity in gliomas, and elucidating signal transduction pathways in proliferation, survival, and invasion in gliomas for the identification of drug target molecules. Her work has garnered over 2,000 citations in cancer cell biology and stem cell biology.

Dr. Rimjhim Agrawal

Co-founder & CTO, BrainSightAI Dr. Rimjhim Agrawal is the Co-founder and Chief Technology Officer of BrainSightAI, a pioneering neurotech startup based in Bengaluru. She holds a PhD in psychiatry with a focus on machine learning for neuroscience, and has over 11 years of experience in neurotech. Her work bridges the complexities of the human brain with the potential of machine learning, exploring how AI can unlock deeper insights into psychiatric disorders like schizophrenia and OCD. Her impactful contributions span 9 patents and more than 10 publications in prestigious journals including Nature, with innovations in brain mapping that assist clinicians in surgeries and treatments. At BrainSightAI, she leads a 30-member team working on generating brain maps for better surgeries and treatment of brain disorders.
