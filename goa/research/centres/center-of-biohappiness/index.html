Prof. M. S. Swaminathan

Prof. M. S. Swaminathan (1925–2023), revered as the Father of India’s Green Revolution, was a pioneering geneticist whose work transformed India from food scarcity to self-sufficiency.

He led premier institutions such as the Indian Agricultural Research Institute and the International Rice Research Institute, and chaired the National Commission on Farmers, championing policies that uplifted smallholder farmers. As founder of the M. S. Swaminathan Research Foundation, he advanced sustainable agriculture and rural development with a strong focus on science serving society.

A global icon of agricultural innovation, he was honoured with the Bharat Ratna, Padma Vibhushan, World Food Prize, Ramon Magsaysay Award, and many other international accolades. His legacy continues to inspire a future where science, sustainability, and social equity move hand in hand.

What We Do at the Centre

  • We work across crop genetics, crop improvement, climate-smart practices, and conservation of traditional seeds and agricultural biodiversity.
  • We enable farmer training, entrepreneurship development, and community-led innovation that keeps farmers at the heart of decision-making.
  • We contribute to agricultural policy, promote inclusive rural development, and foster systems that uplift smallholder and marginalized farming communities.
