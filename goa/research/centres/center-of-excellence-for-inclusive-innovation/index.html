Rationale

WPU GŌA’s transdisciplinary governance model requires Centres of Excellence to act as global validators and integrators within a system that includes Research Institutes (RIs) and Living Learning Scholar Communities (LLSCs).

This centre fulfils this mandate by enabling the university to address complex societal challenges, such as livelihoods, public health, ecological resilience, youth capability, and ethical technology, through integrated, field-embedded and research-driven inclusive innovation.

It links knowledge creation (Research Institutes), community understanding (Living Learning Scholar Communities), and global best practice (Centers of Excellence) to produce scalable, community-centred solutions.

Objectives

  • Advance transdisciplinary research in advanced materials, manufacturing and emergent device systems.
  • Design and develop new materials and affordable green processing methodologies for multiple industrial and societal applications.
  • Foster industry-academia-policy convergence for translational impact.
  • Train next-generation researchers and engineers through experiential learning and hands-on training.
  • Build global partnerships to strengthen India’s role in advanced manufacturing and emergent device development ecosystems.

Mandate

Activities

  • Transdisciplinary research programmes aligned with RI themes, including climate, livelihoods, social technology, and policy.
  • Field immersions and community innovation labs developed in partnership with LLSCs.
  • Teaching and mentorship in inclusive innovation, grassroots methods, frugal engineering, and enterprise.
  • Grassroots innovation scouting (Shodh Yatra-inspired engagements) in collaboration with community networks.
  • Social enterprise incubation for youth-led and community-driven ventures.
  • Policy and systems design labs collaborating with government and civic organisations.

Operational Plan

Mashelkar–Gupta Annual Innovation Lecture Series

Population-Scale Impact Summit bringing together researchers, practitioners, and policymakers

Summer/winter schools on frugal innovation, livelihoods, and social systems

LLSC-embedded community challenges and problem-solving studios

RI-aligned applied research studios for capstone programmes

Grassroots immersions across Goa and India

Fellowships for innovators, youth, and community researchers

White papers and toolkits on MLM, grassroots models, and systems innovation

Partnerships and Alliances

Industry CSR divisions, inclusive-tech companies, digital public infrastructure initiatives, social-impact incubators.

Academia Institutions specialising in inclusive innovation, public policy, sustainability, systems thinking, and design research.

Government State innovation missions, rural livelihood programmes, ministries (MSME, Environment, Education), local government bodies.

Community and Civil Society SHGs, cooperatives, grassroots innovators, NGOs, and rural and tribal communities linked to LLSCs.

Global Networks Frugal innovation labs, grassroots innovation networks, social enterprise ecosystems.

Outcomes and Impact

  • Establishment of a national model for frugal and grassroots inclusive innovation
  • Creation of integrated, field-grounded, RI-aligned research outputs
  • Youth trained as integrated solution architects, capable of transforming systems
  • Strong community partnerships through LLSC structures
  • Public systems and policies influenced through evidence-based insights
  • Scaling of community-owned enterprises, cooperatives, and livelihood models
  • Enhanced global visibility for WPU GŌA’s transdisciplinary mission

Sushil Borde

Head, Reliance Innovation Leadership Centre, Reliance Industries Ltd. Sushil Borde is the Head of the Reliance Innovation Leadership Centre at Reliance Industries Limited, India's largest private sector company. He spearheads strategic initiatives in the Chairman's office of Reliance Industries and has also led key initiatives including Mumbai Indians and was part of the founding teams of Reliance Foundation and Jio Institute. He convened the Reliance Innovation Council of Nobel Laureates and global thought leaders and holds a patent for an innovation platform. He spent seven years in South Africa leading the Advanced Manufacturing Technology Strategy, deploying public funds in technology programmes to make South Africa's manufacturing sector globally competitive. He formerly served as President of the Fab Foundation at MIT, advancing the global 'Invention as Aid' mission through Fab Labs, and is also President of the Anjani Mashelkar Foundation, driving affordable, high-tech healthcare innovations.
