To drive international research and innovation where human creativity, cognition, and artificial intelligence converge creating human-focused AI solutions, structures, and creative approaches that strengthen cross-cultural understanding, conflict resolution, and community welfare through interdisciplinary partnerships.

Prof. Fernando Garibay

Founder and Chairman, The Garibay Institute GRAMMY® & BMI Award Winning Producer Professor, Thunderbird School of Global Management Professor, Herberger ASU Visiting lecturer at Harvard, MIT, Stanford

This centre is established to honour the transformative contributions of the Garibay Institute of Soft Power & Public Diplomacy, led by its Founder and Chairman, Prof. Fernando Garibay. The Garibay Institute has pioneered global discourse on the irreducible human spirit, creative intelligence, and the ethical integration of artificial intelligence at the intersection of art, technology, and human expression. This centre extends that institutional legacy by championing frameworks that position human imagination, emotion, and cognition at the heart of AI development, uniting artists, choreographers, technologists, cognitive scientists, and diplomats in pursuit of research that amplifies rather than replaces humanity’s collective pursuit of meaning and well-being.

Focus Areas

The Immutability of Human Creativity

Explores imagination, cognition, neuro-creativity, and non-replicable aspects of consciousness

Human-Centred Artificial Intelligence

Explores explainability, ethics, emotional AI, and flourishing beyond computation

Creative Industries and Culture-Tech

Explores XR, immersive arts, creative computing, performance technology

Public Diplomacy and Soft Power as Human Resonance

Explores empathy, cultural resonance, moral imagination in diplomacy, and art as policy

Embodied Intelligence and Movement Studies

Explores dance, choreography, kinesthetic creativity, somatic knowledge

Society, Emotion and Sense Literacy

Explores creative expression for wellbeing, collective meaning-making

Transdisciplinary Creative Systems

Explores art–science–technology convergence, uniquely human traits in automated systems

Activities

  • Fundamental and applied research on human creativity beyond computation, including embodied cognition.
  • Sponsored research projects examining AI’s relationship to consciousness, creativity, and movement.
  • Creative-AI labs, prototyping studios, choreographic research, and field-based studies.
  • Capacity building for faculty, scholars, practitioners, choreographers, and future cultural diplomats.
  • International collaborations with universities, studios, performing arts institutions, and AI institutes.
  • Residencies for artists, choreographers, dancers, designers, technologists, cultural diplomats, and creative thinkers.

Members

Paul Gordon Emerson

Co-Founder, Executive & Co-Artistic Director, Company | E Paul Gordon Emerson is the Co-Founder, Executive, and Co-Artistic Director of Company | E, a Washington, DC-based performing arts organisation at the intersection of dance, culture, and diplomacy. A native New Yorker, he previously served as Legislative Director on Capitol Hill and pursued a Master's degree in Security Policy Studies at George Washington University before discovering a passion for dance, combining his foreign policy interests with culture. He has led Company | E on international cultural engagements in partnership with the U.S. Department of State across 50+ countries, and directs the company's innovations in 3D world building and the intersection of art, education, and technology, including a VR SuperMuseum in development with the State Department.

Prof. James Cooper

Professor, California Western School of Law Fellow, Singapore University of Social Sciences (SUSS) Prof. James Cooper is a Professor of Law and Director of International Legal Studies at California Western School of Law, San Diego, and a Research Fellow at the Singapore University of Social Sciences (SUSS). A Cambridge University-trained Barrister and Solicitor, he began his career with Baker & McKenzie and has held faculty positions at the University of Strathclyde, Glasgow, and the University of Toronto Faculty of Law. He has consulted for the U.S. Departments of Justice and State, the Organisation of American States, and numerous governments and international agencies across Latin America and Asia. His current research focuses on the legal regulation of emerging technologies including blockchain, artificial intelligence, and the metaverse, and he has been widely profiled in publications such as the Wall Street Journal, Time Magazine, and the New York Times.

Prof. Ronald C. Gunnell

President, The Garibay Institute Founder & Global Chairman, Truth Alone Triumphs - Global Enterprises, LLC Prof. Ronald C. Gunnell is President of the Garibay Institute for Soft Power and Public Diplomacy and Founder & Global Chairman of Truth Alone Triumphs – Global Enterprises, LLC. The Los Angeles-based Garibay Institute, founded by musical icon Fernando Garibay, engages a network spanning 192 countries and collaborates with leading universities including Harvard, MIT, Stanford, Oxford, and Cambridge. Gunnell holds joint professorial appointments with the Thunderbird School of Global Management and the Herberger Institute for Design and the Arts. His enterprise specialises in global consulting and dialogue bridge-building across the United States, Egypt, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Brazil, and India. His academic interests span cross-cultural leadership, creative diplomacy, design thinking in governance, and the arts–international development nexus, with lectures delivered across continents and contributions to global policy forums.
