To drive international research and innovation where human creativity, cognition, and artificial intelligence converge creating human-focused AI solutions, structures, and creative approaches that strengthen cross-cultural understanding, conflict resolution, and community welfare through interdisciplinary partnerships.

Prof. Fernando Garibay

Founder and Chairman, The Garibay Institute GRAMMY® & BMI Award Winning Producer Professor, Thunderbird School of Global Management Professor, Herberger ASU Visiting lecturer at Harvard, MIT, Stanford

This centre is established to honour the transformative contributions of the Garibay Institute of Soft Power & Public Diplomacy, led by its Founder and Chairman, Prof. Fernando Garibay. The Garibay Institute has pioneered global discourse on the irreducible human spirit, creative intelligence, and the ethical integration of artificial intelligence at the intersection of art, technology, and human expression. This centre extends that institutional legacy by championing frameworks that position human imagination, emotion, and cognition at the heart of AI development, uniting artists, choreographers, technologists, cognitive scientists, and diplomats in pursuit of research that amplifies rather than replaces humanity’s collective pursuit of meaning and well-being.

Focus Areas

The Immutability of Human Creativity

Explores imagination, cognition, neuro-creativity, and non-replicable aspects of consciousness

Human-Centred Artificial Intelligence

Explores explainability, ethics, emotional AI, and flourishing beyond computation

Creative Industries and Culture-Tech

Explores XR, immersive arts, creative computing, performance technology

Public Diplomacy and Soft Power as Human Resonance

Explores empathy, cultural resonance, moral imagination in diplomacy, and art as policy

Embodied Intelligence and Movement Studies

Explores dance, choreography, kinesthetic creativity, somatic knowledge

Society, Emotion and Sense Literacy

Explores creative expression for wellbeing, collective meaning-making

Transdisciplinary Creative Systems

Explores art–science–technology convergence, uniquely human traits in automated systems

Activities

  • Fundamental and applied research on human creativity beyond computation, including embodied cognition.
  • Sponsored research projects examining AI’s relationship to consciousness, creativity, and movement.
  • Creative-AI labs, prototyping studios, choreographic research, and field-based studies.
  • Capacity building for faculty, scholars, practitioners, choreographers, and future cultural diplomats.
  • International collaborations with universities, studios, performing arts institutions, and AI institutes.
  • Residencies for artists, choreographers, dancers, designers, technologists, cultural diplomats, and creative thinkers.
