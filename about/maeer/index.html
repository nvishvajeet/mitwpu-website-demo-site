MAEER

MIT began its academic journey under the Maharashtra Academy of Engineering and Educational Research (MAEER) in 1983. MIT World Peace University carries that educational legacy.

World Peace Centre · Alandi

Since 1998

UNESCO Chair and the culture of peace

The World Peace Centre at Alandi was established in 1996. A UNESCO Chair in Peace, Human Rights and Democracy was established at MAEER in 1998, with Prof. Dr. Vishwanath D. Karad as chairholder. In 2019, UNESCO New Delhi and MIT-WPU jointly organised the Second Annual Meeting of UNESCO Chairs in India in Pune.

Legacy

Four decades of education

A concise record drawn from the university’s published history.

  1. 1981–1983

    An institution conceived

    MIT was conceived as an institution dedicated to innovation and excellence in education.

  2. 1983

    The inaugural year

    MIT began its academic journey and welcomed its first Electronics and Telecommunication students.

  3. 1996–2003

    World Peace Centre and UNESCO Chair

    The World Peace Centre at Alandi was established in 1996. A UNESCO Chair in Peace, Human Rights and Democracy followed in 1998, and the institution continued its public work in education and peace.

  4. 2004–2014

    New national initiatives

    The Bharat Asmita National Awards, Vishwashanti Gurukul, and Bharatiya Chhatra Sansad expanded the institution’s public work.

  5. 2015–2019

    Expanding horizons

    The institution expanded its educational presence, and the World Peace Dome at Loni, Pune, became a symbol of its commitment to peace.

  6. Since 2021

    A university in transformation

    MIT-WPU has continued its digital transformation while carrying forward its commitment to education, peace, and innovation.
