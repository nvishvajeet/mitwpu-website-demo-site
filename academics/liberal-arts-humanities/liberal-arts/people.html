Prof. Anjoo Sharan Upadhyaya

Distinguish Professor & Senior Advisor

Dr. Anjoo Sharan Upadhyaya is a distinguished Political Scientist from Banaras Hindu University (BHU), where she served over four decades in pivotal roles including Senior Professor and Chair of Political Science, Dean of Social Sciences, Director of the Centre for Nepal Studies, Director of the Centre for Integrated Rural Development, and Adjunct Professor at the Malaviya Centre for Peace Research. Her academic journey includes a PhD from BHU (1981), MA from Allahabad University (1974), post-doctoral research at the London School of Economics, Woodrow Wilson International Centre for Scholars, Brown University, and an Advanced International Diploma in Conflict Resolution from Uppsala University. She has held prestigious positions such as Research Director at the UN University Institute on Conflict Resolution and Ethnicity (UK), Scholar-in-Residence at Woodrow Wilson Centre (USA), foundational ICCR Chair at Tribhuvan University (Nepal), India Chair by ICSSR New Delhi, and visiting professorships at institutions like Wellesley College, Concordia University, Stanford University, PRIO Oslo, and universities across Sweden, Germany, Australia, Canada, and Poland.

Dr. Shreya Bera

Assistant Professor

Dr. Shreya Bera is an Assistant Professor of English at Dr. Vishwanath Karad MIT World Peace University. She completed her PhD in Literary Studies from the University of Szeged in January 2023 under the prestigious Stipendium Hungaricum Scholarship Programme (2018-2023). She attended multiple international conferences such as the 14th Biennial Conference of the Hungarian Society for the Study of English (HUSSE) in Hungary (2019), 11th Brno International Conference, Czech Republic, ESSE Doctoral Symposium in France (2020), Narratives and Self Ambivalence symposium, SAS, London in 2021 and more. She also co-edited a book titled Critical Essays on Multicultural India: Exploring Inclusiveness and New Humanities published in 2017. She has also authored articles and book reviews in peer-reviewed, and Scopus indexed journals such as Americana e-journal, British and American studies (B.A.S.) journal, The ESSE Messenger etc. She is an established author of two books published in the years of 2017 and 2025.

Dr. Bishnu Prasad Mohapatra

Assistant Professor

Dr. Bishnu Prasad Mohapatra is presently working as Assistant Professor in School of Liberal Arts, Dr. Viswanath Karad MIT-World Peace University, Pune and involved in Teaching and Research in Political Science. Dr. Suchitra Renguntwar

Assistant Professor

Dr. Suchitra Renguntwar (Ajgaonkar) is an Assistant Professor of Psychology in the Department of Liberal Arts at MIT WPU, teaching industrial psychology, psychological assessment in HRD and counselling, and health psychology. She holds a Ph.D. in management and a master’s degree in psychology and is qualified for UGC NET and SET in psychology. She has over six years of experience teaching MBA students and eight years of industry experience. Her empirical research focuses on workforce agility in the information technology industry, learning mechanisms and TQM in the manufacturing industry, and resilience and women empowerment in social enterprises. Her research papers are published in ABDC (B) category journals – International Journal of Organizational Analysis and TQM Journal. Her research interests include socio-cognitive elements in psychology, nudge theory, and collaborative research on the application of psychology to management. She has expertise in mixed-method research, systematic literature review, and qualitative research methods.
