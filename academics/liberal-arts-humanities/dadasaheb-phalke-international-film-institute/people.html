Prof. Hossein Martin Fazeli

Professor of Practice

Hossein Martin Fazeli is a distinguished Iranian writer, producer, and director who has been an A-list filmmaker in North America for many years and currently serves as a Professor of Practice at Dadasaheb Phalke International Film School. With over two decades of experience in producing, directing, and writing fiction and non-fiction television and films, Fazeli has built an illustrious career marked by his extensive international collaborations across more than a dozen countries. His works have been broadcast globally on leading networks such as CBC, BBC, ARTE, and Canal+, earning 37 international awards and critical acclaim.

Maisam Ali

Assistant Professor

Maisam Ali is an independent filmmaker with expertise in both short and feature films. He is a graduate of the Film and Television Institute of India (FTII), majoring in Film Direction. His short films have been exhibited and honoured in France, the United States, the United Arab Emirates, and India. His feature film In Retreat was the first Indian film to be included in the ACID sidebar section at the Cannes Film Festival 2024, marking a 32-year milestone. The film was screened at the Busan International Film Festival, Sao Paulo Film Festival, Galway Film Festival, Moscow International Film Festival, and Mumbai Film Festival, among others. In addition, he has been teaching filmmaking at several institutions in India. He is presently overseeing Direction practices at Dadasaheb International Film School in the position of Assistant Professor.

Dr. Abhijith B

Assistant Professor

Abhijith B serves as an Assistant Professor at the Dadasaheb Phalke International Film Institute, MIT-WPU. He holds a PhD in Cultural Studies from The English and Foreign Languages University, Hyderabad. His study concentrates on contemporary Malayalam cinema, particularly addressing issues of form, space, and subjectivity. By prioritising the ideology of form as a primary theoretical framework, his work analyses the historical transformations influencing Indian cinema in a broader context. His study examines the intertwining of geography and story as a crucial indicator of cinema's social component in the contemporary era, especially in the absence of concrete social markers under the neoliberal context. His study has been published in peer-reviewed journals, and he is presently examining the correlation between contemporary genre forms and the issue of political federalism in Indian cinema.

Dr. Namrata Sathe

Assistant Professor

Dr. Namrata Sathe is an Assistant Professor at the Dadasaheb Phalke International Film Institute, MIT-WPU. She holds a PhD in Media Studies from Southern Illinois University, Carbondale. Her book, The Neoliberal Self in Bollywood: Cinema, Popular Culture, and Identity (Intellect Books, 2024), examines the consequences of unbridled expansion of neoliberal values within India through the lens of popular film and culture. Her scholarship has been published in prominent academic journals including Jump Cut, Journal of Cinema and Media Studies, and New Review of Film and Television Studies , as well as in edited collections on Indian cinema and media. She serves as the Assistant Editor for the SCOPUS-listed academic journal Studies in South Asian Film and Media . Her research interests include feminist media studies, queer studies, sexuality studies, and popular culture.

Prathamesh Sonawani

Teaching Associate

Prathamesh Sonawani believes teaching is about awakening perception, not just delivering information. In his classes, he encourages students to listen, observe, and think critically about sound, performance, and storytelling as lived experiences, not just technical subjects. His motivation comes from seeing learners discover their own artistic voice and confidence. He blends practical industry insight with creative exploration, so students are prepared for both professional realities and meaningful expression. At MIT-WPU, he hopes to contribute to an environment where artistic skill, cultural awareness, and emotional intelligence grow together in thoughtful, socially aware creators.
