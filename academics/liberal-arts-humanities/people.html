Prof. Anjoo Sharan Upadhyaya

Distinguish Professor & Senior Advisor

Dr. Anjoo Sharan Upadhyaya is a distinguished Political Scientist from Banaras Hindu University (BHU), where she served over four decades in pivotal roles including Senior Professor and Chair of Political Science, Dean of Social Sciences, Director of the Centre for Nepal Studies, Director of the Centre for Integrated Rural Development, and Adjunct Professor at the Malaviya Centre for Peace Research. Her academic journey includes a PhD from BHU (1981), MA from Allahabad University (1974), post-doctoral research at the London School of Economics, Woodrow Wilson International Centre for Scholars, Brown University, and an Advanced International Diploma in Conflict Resolution from Uppsala University. She has held prestigious positions such as Research Director at the UN University Institute on Conflict Resolution and Ethnicity (UK), Scholar-in-Residence at Woodrow Wilson Centre (USA), foundational ICCR Chair at Tribhuvan University (Nepal), India Chair by ICSSR New Delhi, and visiting professorships at institutions like Wellesley College, Concordia University, Stanford University, PRIO Oslo, and universities across Sweden, Germany, Australia, Canada, and Poland.

Dr. Abhijith B

Assistant Professor

Abhijith B serves as an Assistant Professor at the Dadasaheb Phalke International Film Institute, MIT-WPU. He holds a PhD in Cultural Studies from The English and Foreign Languages University, Hyderabad. His study concentrates on contemporary Malayalam cinema, particularly addressing issues of form, space, and subjectivity. By prioritising the ideology of form as a primary theoretical framework, his work analyses the historical transformations influencing Indian cinema in a broader context. His study examines the intertwining of geography and story as a crucial indicator of cinema's social component in the contemporary era, especially in the absence of concrete social markers under the neoliberal context. His study has been published in peer-reviewed journals, and he is presently examining the correlation between contemporary genre forms and the issue of political federalism in Indian cinema.

Dr. Namrata Sathe

Assistant Professor

Dr. Namrata Sathe is an Assistant Professor at the Dadasaheb Phalke International Film Institute, MIT-WPU. She holds a PhD in Media Studies from Southern Illinois University, Carbondale. Her book, The Neoliberal Self in Bollywood: Cinema, Popular Culture, and Identity (Intellect Books, 2024), examines the consequences of unbridled expansion of neoliberal values within India through the lens of popular film and culture. Her scholarship has been published in prominent academic journals including Jump Cut, Journal of Cinema and Media Studies, and New Review of Film and Television Studies , as well as in edited collections on Indian cinema and media. She serves as the Assistant Editor for the SCOPUS-listed academic journal Studies in South Asian Film and Media . Her research interests include feminist media studies, queer studies, sexuality studies, and popular culture.
