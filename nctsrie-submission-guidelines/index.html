Nctsrie Submission Guidelines

National Conference Towards a Self-Reliant and Inclusive Economy for Viksit Bharat 2047

Full Research Paper: Original theoretical, empirical, or policy-oriented research papers presenting objectives, methodology, analysis, findings, and implications (4,000–6,000 words).

  • Full Research Paper : Original theoretical, empirical, or policy-oriented research papers presenting objectives, methodology, analysis, findings, and implications (4,000–6,000 words).
  • Extended Abstract : Concise submission highlighting research objectives, methodology, key findings, and implications of ongoing or completed studies (1,000–1,500 words).
  • Article : Analytical or practice-oriented articles discussing contemporary issues, challenges, and policy perspectives related to GST reforms and sectoral impact. Case Study: Original real-world case studies focusing on GST implementation, sectoral challenges, compliance issues, and decision-making insights.
  • Original and unpublished research papers, conceptual papers, case studies, articles, and opinion pieces are invited from academicians, researchers, industry professionals, and practitioners.
  • Papers should be theoretical, empirical, or policy-oriented and aligned with the conference theme and sub-themes.
  • Papers must be typed in MS Word, Times New Roman, 12-point font, 1.5 line spacing, with APA referencing style.
  • A separate title page should include:
  • Title of the paper
  • Author(s) name(s)
  • Institutional affiliation
  • Email ID and contact number
  • All submissions will undergo a peer-review process.
