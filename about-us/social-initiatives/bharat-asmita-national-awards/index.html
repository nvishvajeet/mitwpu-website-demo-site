The Bharat Asmita National Awards, initiated in 2005 by MAEER's MIT Group of Institutions, Pune, honors individuals contributing significantly to Nation Building in the realms of management, mass awakening, and Indian Parliamentary Practices. This esteemed award has grown in reputation and stature over the years. The board of trustees and Awards Selection Committee maintain high standards in recognizing exceptional contributions

The committee invites over 15,000 nominations from diverse sectors, including government officials, legislators, bureaucrats, judiciary, media, educators, entertainers, writers, and NGOs

The awards consist of three categories, with one award presented in each. In exceptional cases, a shared award may be given to two equally deserving individuals in a given year. The scope of the award encompasses all states and union territories throughout India.

Background

MAEER’s MIT Group of Institutes was established by Professor (Dr.) Vishwanath D. Karad, a distinguished educator, philosopher, a committed instructor, and a devoted social worker. He was a trailblazer in introducing the policies of educational privatization in the state of Maharashtra. The institution was founded in 1983 under the umbrella of the Maharashtra Academy of Engineering and Educational Research (MAEER)

MAEER’s MIT Pune has been at the forefront of education, boasting a network of 68 institutes with over 55,000 students across diverse disciplines, including Engineering, Medicine, Management, Pharmacy, Information Technology, Foreign Languages, Government studies, and more. Additionally, it serves as the host for the UNESCO Chair for Human Rights, Democracy, and Peace

The Scientific Advisory Council of MIT Pune comprises distinguished individuals such as Dr. R. A. Mashelkar, Dr. Vijay Bhatkar, Dr. V. L. Kelkar, Professor S. V. Patankar, and Mr. Arvind Kirloskar, among others

Objectives

MAEER's MIT Group of Institutions recognises the nation's growth lies in inspiring the younger generation of Indians. They aim to acknowledge the exceptional achievements of pioneers who, through their intellect, are setting new standards in management education, public awareness through various means like literature, art, cinema, media, social service, or NGOs, and parliamentary representation. To honour excellence, they have instituted the "Bharat Asmita National Awards," which will bring together a community with a shared aspiration of advancing India's development based on strong ethics and growth practices. Their collective efforts will elevate performance standards and work towards achieving a developed and globally competitive India

  • To recognize and honor individuals who have made remarkable contributions to the upliftment and betterment of Indian society and the nation as a whole
  • To create awareness and appreciation for the diverse contributions that people from all walks of life make to the nation's growth and well-being
  • To celebrate and acknowledge the efforts of those who have significantly impacted various facets of Indian life, including education, politics, and public service
  • To foster a sense of national pride and unity by highlighting the exceptional work done by individuals in different fields and sectors
  • To encourage and inspire individuals and organizations to engage in nation-building activities and work towards the progress and development of the country.
  • To promote and preserve the rich cultural heritage, values, and traditions of India
  • To encourage continued dedication and commitment to the welfare and progress of the nation.
