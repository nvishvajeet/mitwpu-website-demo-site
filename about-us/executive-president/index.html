Executive President

Carrying forward a glorious legacy of Revered Prof (Dr.) Vishwanath D Karad, Dr. Rahul Vishwanath Karad serves as the Managing Trustee & Executive President of MAEER’S MIT Group of Institutions, which encompasses 70+ institutes and nurtures over 80,000 students.

An engineering graduate, a postgraduate in management from Cardiff University, UK, and an alumnus of Harvard Business School, USA, Dr. Rahul Karad is dedicated to nation-building through youth empowerment. His vision is to help young minds realise their full potential through holistic, value-based education.

MIT-WPU School of Government

In 2005, Dr. Rahul Karad founded the MIT-WPU School of Government (MIT-SOG) with the mission of shaping future political leaders who embody ethics, spirituality, and leadership. The institute offers India’s first-of-its-kind two-year full-time Master’s Programme in Political Leadership & Governance, equipping students with expertise in political strategy, electoral processes, governance, international relations, media, and NGO work.

Bharatiya Chhatra Sansad

A pioneering youth platform, Bharatiya Chhatra Sansad (BCS) aims to integrate young voices into India’s political and social democracy. It provides practical leadership training to groom students into future leaders. Every year, the BCS Summit hosts over 12,000 students from 400+ colleges across India, fostering discussions on nation-building, governance, and civic responsibility.

National Legislators' Conference (NLC) Bharat

The National Legislators’ Conference Bharat (NLC Bharat) is an avenue for Members of Legislative Assemblies (MLAs) and Members of Legislative Councils (MLCs) across the nation to gather at one place and exchange ideas in this historic conference. The first edition of NLC Bharat took place in Mumbai from 15 to 17 June 2023. MLAs and MLCs from across the political parties and from all the States and UTs were provided with a unique opportunity to interact with each other. The first edition of the conference was conducted with the objectives of cross-learning and enhancement of legislative effectiveness at its core. The legislators acquired experiential learnings through thematic discussions along with a mix of thoughtfully curated large-format plenary sessions, brainstorming parallel sessions amongst the legislators, a showcase of States’ and UTs’ pavilions and commendable practices, and extensive informal interactions amongst the legislators, party leaders, Living Icons of Bharat and CSR representatives.
