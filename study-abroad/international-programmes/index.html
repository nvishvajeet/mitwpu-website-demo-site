Study Abroad

International Programmes

A summer/winter school is a form of education provided by MIT-WPU together with an international partner university.

Study Abroad

This exploratory programme is a non-credit engagement and constitutes of 3 mentioned benefits

  • Domain Knowledge
  • Cross Cultural Exposure
  • Industrial Exposure

The university also engages its students from specific schools and provisions their travel to international host universities for an immersion programme also called Summer/Winter School (depending on the time of the year) for the duration of 2-3 weeks. MIT-WPU offers this international exposure to all the students in the form of 2–3-week academic engagement, international expo, international conferences, or cultural fests.

Studying abroad will allow students to experience different cultures and perspectives that includes meeting new people. Students get exposure to step out of their comfort zone and challenge unfamiliar situations. MIT-WPU’s Student Exchange Programmes enable students to study abroad at one of our international partner institutions/universities.

MIT-WPU ensures to provide enriching experience and global exposure to students for at least two weeks without compromising on their academics. MIT-WPU is very keen on delivering this experience to its students through their partner institutions/universities across the globe.

In this programme students go to foreign institutions/universities for one or two consecutive semesters with respect to their specialisation with the appropriate credit mapping and exchange of academics.

Students get exposure through semester abroad programme that includes visiting new places, experiencing new cultures, and making new friends around the world. This programme will allow the students to gain an international experience and develop highly valued skills such as intercultural communication, foreign languages, getting exposed to new cultures, critical thinking and problem-solving abilities.
