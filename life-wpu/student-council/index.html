Student Election

Student elections at MIT-WPU are a crucial opportunity for our student body to exercise their democratic rights and actively shape the future of our university community. Through these elections, students have the chance to select their representatives who will advocate for their interests, voice their concerns, and work towards enhancing the overall student experience. The university encourages all students to participate in this democratic process, as it plays a vital role in ensuring that our university continues to evolve in a way that reflects the aspirations and needs of its diverse student population.

Perks and Benefits

  • The format of the certificate of appreciation is attached with emphasis on necessary details, further design shall be done by the Media Department
  • Each student council member shall be presented with a certificate of appreciation from the University at the end of the council term. during the formation of the new student council.

Campaigning Guidelines

There is a certain Code of Conduct that applies to campaigning during the elections. They are:

  • No candidates shall be permitted to make use of printed posters, printed pamphlets or any other printed material for the purpose for canvassing. No physical canvassing will be allowed under all circumstances.
  • No candidate shall be permitted to carry out processions, or public meetings, or in any way canvass or distribute propaganda material inside or outside the university campus.
  • No candidate shall, nor shall his/her supporters, deface or cause any destruction to any property of the university, for any purpose or reason, whatsoever. In case of such incident all candidates shall be held jointly and severally liable for such destruction/defacing university property and disciplinary actions will be initiated against that or those candidates.
  • Use of loudspeakers, vehicles and animals for the purpose of canvassing shall be strictly prohibited.

Result declaration/Investiture Ceremony

Investiture Ceremony will be held to felicitate and announce the Council Members in presence of the official authorities, (i.e. MIT-WPU President, Executive President, Vice Chancellor, Provost, Registrar and Deans) as available.

An official email would be sent to all students of all schools under the MIT-WPU umbrella by Dean Student’s Affairs / HoS, containing the names, post and contact numbers of all the council members. Boards displaying elected member’s names would be put up within a week of the elections. Dean Student’s Affairs shall coordinate with the media for the boards.

Grievance Redressal

During the election process, there shall be a 'Grievances Redressal Committee' constituted by the Vice Chancellor with the Dean (Student Affairs) as its chairman. In addition, there shall be two senior members of the staff (one teaching staff and one administrative staff) nominated by the Vice Chancellor, two out-going student council members (nominated by the Student Council) preferably, one boy and one girl. This committee shall be in place during the election process or till all the grievances received during the process are reviewed and settled.

FAQs

Q1: How does the university assure fair practice during voting?

Ans: The university authorities shall appoint impartial observers. If the candidates have any specific complaint or problem regarding the conduct of the elections; they may bring the same to the notice of the observer. Observers shall also be appointed to oversee the process of nomination for student representatives.

Q2: My complaint to the Grievance Redressal Committee was dismissed. What could be the reasons?

Ans: The Grievance Redressal Committee may dismiss a complaint if:

  • The complaint was not filed within the time frame prescribed in point above.
  • The complaint fails to state a cause of action for which relief may be granted.
  • The complaint has not and/or likely will not suffer any injury or damage.

Q3: Is there a list of Student Council positions? Are positions added or removed?

Ans: The different positions in the student council that need to be selected are as follows. Every year these positions may be expanded as per the requirement of the university depending upon student strength.

  • Sports Secretary
