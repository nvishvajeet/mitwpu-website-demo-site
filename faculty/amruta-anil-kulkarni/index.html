Faculty

Ms. Amruta Anil Kulkarni

Ms. Amruta Anil Kulkarni is a educator background in Sanskrit language, Vedic literature, and Indology. Currently pursuing her PhD in Vedic literature from MIT ADT,Pune, she holds a Master's degree in Sanskrit Grammar and is also pursuing a Master's in Indology. She has cleared both the National Eligibility Test (NET) and State Eligibility Test (SET) in Sanskrit.

Assistant Professor

Bio

Currently an Assistant Professor at the School of Peace Studies, MIT-World Peace University, Pune, with over 7 years of experience in Sanskrit research and teaching. Expertise in Indology and Ancient Indian Culture, with experience as Visiting Faculty at Bhishma Indic Studies. Designed syllabi on Ancient Indian Knowledge Systems and contributed articles to magazines. Certified NLP-Huna Trainer and Practitioner.

Pursuing PhD in Sanskrit at MIT-ADT University, qualified NET (2018) and SET (2016), and holds MA in Sanskrit Grammar and Indology. Former Research Assistant at Vaidika Samshodhana Mandala with experience in manuscript handling and proofreading Kanva Shatapatha Brahmana. Designed courses for Peace Studies at MIT-WPU and Bhishma Indic Studies. Published research papers on Sahityashastra and Kautilya's Understanding of Peace. Actively contributed to cultural projects, including collaborations with Dr. Bhagyalata Pataskar and Charudatta Aphale Maharaj.

Research Area Indology, Sanskrit, German language, IKS
