Bio

Mr. Harshal Bhusari is associated with the MIT School of Government, where he engages in the study and dissemination of political science, international relations, and governance. He holds a graduate degree in Civil Engineering, a postgraduate degree in Political Science & International Relations from Pune University, and a Master’s degree from MIT School of Government. He also earned an English certificate from the University of Cambridge and has cleared the Maharashtra SET in 2018 and UGC NET twice, in 2019 and 2022.

A state-level orator with over a decade of experience, Harshal has authored the book “Game of Power: Navigating 48 Laws in India”, a reinterpretation of Robert Greene’s “The 48 Laws of Power” that draws on India’s historical and political wisdom. He is frequently invited to podcasts, popular talk shows, and leading coaching institutes to speak on contemporary Indian politics, post-independence political developments, and the Indian Constitution.

In addition, Harshal runs a YouTube channel dedicated to explaining power politics and enhancing political literacy by breaking down complex political concepts into accessible case studies. Through his research, writing, and public engagement, he works to make political knowledge more approachable and to foster informed citizenry.
