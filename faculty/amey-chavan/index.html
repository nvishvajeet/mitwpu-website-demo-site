Bio

Amey Chavan is associated with the MIT School of Government and NLC Bharat, where he works as an Assistant Manager. He is part of a team that designs and delivers academic and training programs to help legislators better understand parliamentary work, governance, and public discourse.

He has been actively involved in planning and conducting policy discussions, forums, and workshops on topics like elections, legislative processes, and international relations.

Amey has also contributed to academic writing, including a book chapter titled “Redefining Digital Democracy: Governance in the 21st Century.” He is interested in techno-governance, studying how technology and public policy work together. His work focuses on digital regulation, platform governance, and the responsible use of new technologies. He is especially interested in how governments can balance innovation with accountability in a data-driven world.
