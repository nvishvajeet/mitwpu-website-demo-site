Bio

Dr. Amrin Noor is an Assistant Professor of Economics at the Ram Charan School of Leadership, Dr. Vishwanath Karad MIT World Peace University, Pune. With over a decade of academic experience, she specializes in international trade, development economics, agricultural economics, labour studies, and entrepreneurship. Her research focuses on gender and labour market dynamics, global trade integration, innovation, and the economic implications of artificial intelligence. She is committed to experiential learning, research- driven teaching, and fostering industry-academia engagement in alignment with MIT-WPU’s vision of value-based education and societal transformation.

Educational Qualifications Ph.D. in Economics, Barkatullah University, Bhopal (2022) Thesis: Growth of India's Foreign Trade and its Regional Linkages

Master of Business Economics, Barkatullah University (2011)

Bachelor of Commerce (Computer Applications), Barkatullah University (2008)

Areas of Expertise International Trade and Globalization, Agricultural and Rural Economics, Development Economics, Labour Economics and Gender Studies, Entrepreneurship and Innovation, Behavioural Economics, Indian Economy and Public Policy, and Research Methodology and Data Analytics.

Teaching Experience More than 10 years of teaching experience in higher education across undergraduate and postgraduate programmes. Currently serving as Assistant Professor at MIT-WPU and previously associated with Jagran Lakecity University, National Law Institute University, and Saifia Arts, Commerce and Law College.

Industry / Research Experience Extensive experience in academic research, student career development, institutional administration, curriculum development, and research supervision. Research interests include international trade, agricultural economics, labour markets, entrepreneurship, gender studies, AI-driven economic transformation, and development policy.
