WPU School of Law

Discover the School of Law at MIT-WPU, where legal education integrates practical insights with theoretical knowledge. The institution is committed to equipping students with a strong academic foundation, cultivating ethical principles, and preparing them for a variety of legal vocations.

About

The School of Law at MIT-WPU was founded in 2018, marking a new chapter in the institution’s history. In just three years, it has earned its reputation as the Leading School of Law in India. The School of Law focuses on a value-driven approach to legal education, incorporating interactive sessions, hands-on practical training, moot court competitions, classroom presentations, and industry-centric assignments. Going beyond the conventional, the school enriches the learning experience with field trips, rural immersion programmes, internships, national and international study tours, and immersive projects that provide a profound understanding of society and the pivotal role of legal professionals. The curriculum stands out with its unique blend of courses covering world peace and real-world research projects while fostering a holistic educational journey. Co-curricular and extracurricular activities hold a significant place, ensuring a well-rounded education. The School of Law at MIT-WPU aims to instil a comprehensive perspective on the importance of legal education in the modern world and prepares its students for diverse and rewarding career paths.

Overview

Our Objectives

  • To impart legal education at par with global standards
  • To encourage students in developing habits of legal reasoning, independent thought, and a spirit of inquiry
  • To develop skills in legal research and analysis as they is extremely important for a practicing lawyer
  • To offer students a wide variety of Law subjects and optional papers to choose from
  • To help students strengthen their foundations of legal knowledge
  • To expose the students to the need for and importance of legal education in the contemporary world
  • To encourage the use of legal education for the development and progress of society
