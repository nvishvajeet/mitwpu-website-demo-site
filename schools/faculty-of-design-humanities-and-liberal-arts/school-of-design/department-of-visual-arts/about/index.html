Department of Visual Arts

Learn about MIT-WPU's Department of Visual Arts, which is dedicated to fostering creativity and artistic development. The department offers a comprehensive curriculum focused on both traditional and contemporary art practices, preparing students for diverse careers in the visual arts field. With expert faculty and state-of-the-art facilities, the department provides an enriching learning experience.

About

The Department of Visual Arts at MIT-WPU School of Design offers a compelling array of undergraduate and postgraduate programmes meticulously tailored for aspiring creatives eager to establish their presence in the burgeoning creative sector. These programmes are the result of a collaborative effort between industry practitioners and esteemed academicians, ensuring a dynamic fusion of traditional artistic principles and contemporary technology-driven practices.

To immerse students in the ever-evolving landscape of the creative industry, the academic calendar incorporates a diverse range of experiences. These include educational excursions to industry sites, faculty-guided projects, as well as enriching visits to museums and art galleries. This multifaceted curriculum artfully blends theoretical instruction with hands-on experiential learning, equipping students with a profound understanding of art mediums, materials, composition, proportions, and colour theory. Furthermore, it imparts invaluable skills such as time and financial management, perspective mapping, and creative and design thinking. At its core, these programmes instil in students the capacity to engage in independent, ethical, and contextually informed artistic practice. This practice is characterised by a keen focus on functionality, which ultimately prepares students for a diverse array of rewarding careers, such as Graphic Designer, Advertising Expert, Illustrator, Photographer, and many more. In essence, the Department of Visual Arts at MIT World Peace University’s School of Design stands as a beacon for aspiring artists and designers, offering a holistic education that bridges the gap between artistic tradition and contemporary industry.

Our Objectives

  • To instill an eye for identifying and understanding Visual art and its responsibility to the cultural environment.
  • To nurture the ability to engage in independent and contextually informed Applied Art practices.
  • To build a capacity for critical evaluation of relevant scholarly literature and Applied Art practice.
