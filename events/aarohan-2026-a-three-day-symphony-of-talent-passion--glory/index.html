AAROHAN 2026: A Three-Day Symphony of Talent, Passion & Glory

Experience the magic of Aarohan 2026 at MIT-WPU! A three-day symphony of talent, passion, and glory featuring celebrity pro-nights, high-octane competitions, and elite cultural showcases. Don’t miss Pune's most anticipated college fest. Register now!

Some moments don't just happen — they're built, note by note, move by move, story by story. And from 24-26 February 2026, MIT-WPU Pune did exactly that. AAROHAN returned with its 14th edition, and Pune's largest cultural extravaganza proved once again, that it only gets louder, grander, and more electrifying with every passing year.

Entirely conceptualised and brought to life by the students of MIT-WPU Pune, AAROHAN 2026 was a three-day celebration of raw talent, creative fire, and unbridled energy — where every performance told a story and every stage became a world of its own. Months of passionate preparation, meticulous planning, and relentless rehearsals converged into one spectacular explosion of culture and creativity across campus.

Day 1 Set the tone with Dastak, where powerful street narratives shook the ground, and the thunderous Band Wars, where music bands went head-to-head in an epic showdown of sound and skill.

Day 2 Slowed things down — beautifully. Art Maestro, the Fine Arts Competition where imagination took the lead, brought out the best in Drawing, Portrait Painting, and more, while Humming drew out the finest solo vocalists and Theatre Fest reminded everyone why live theatre never gets old.

Day 3 was nothing short of spectacular — Glamour Nova turned the campus into a runway, Inner Flame spotlighted solo dancers at their expressive best, and a high-octane Group Dance finale judged by Dharmesh Yelande, Indian Dancer and Choreographer, brought the crowd to its feet before the night crescendoed into a closing celebration that will echo for years.

AAROHAN 2026 was more than performances — it was a living expression of student leadership, creative courage, and the unbreakable spirit of MIT-WPU Pune, and one that will be remembered for years to come.
