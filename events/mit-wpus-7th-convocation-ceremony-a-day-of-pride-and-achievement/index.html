MIT-WPU’s 7th Convocation Ceremony: A Day of Pride and Achievement

Read the full report on MIT-WPU's 7th Convocation Ceremony (Oct 11, 2025). Featuring Hon'ble Dr. C. V. Ananda Bose, degrees conferred on 6,800 graduates, 37 Ph. Ds, and the momentous Governor's Award of Excellence.

“Graduation marks not an end, but the beginning of a lifelong pursuit of knowledge, growth, and purpose."

The 7th Convocation Ceremony of MIT World Peace University, Pune marked a day of pride, achievement, and inspiration as the university celebrated the remarkable success of 6,800 graduates across diverse disciplines.

Graced by eminent leaders including Hon’ble Dr. C. V. Ananda Bose, Governor of West Bengal; Prof. Dr. Ram Charan, acclaimed international business advisor and author; Rev. Prof. Dr. Vishwanath D. Karad, UNESCO Chair Holder and Founder President, MIT-WPU; and Dr. Rahul V. Karad, Executive President, MIT-WPU, the event embodied the spirit of academic excellence and societal contribution.

A momentous highlight was the Governor’s Award of Excellence, instituted by Hon’ble Dr. C. V. Ananda Bose to Rev. Prof. Dr. Vishwanath D. Karad, honouring his extraordinary role in advancing education, fostering peace, and contributing to nation-building.

This year’s accomplishments included 114 Gold Medals, 103 Silver Medals,103 Bronze Medals, and the conferment of 37 Ph.D. degrees — a testament to the dedication and brilliance of MIT-WPU, Pune’s graduating class.

From the radiant smiles of graduates to the proud cheers of their families — every moment reflected achievement, unity, and hope, making the day truly unforgettable.

Beyond being an academic milestone, the convocation was a celebration of human potential, visionary leadership, and world peace. Heartiest congratulations to the Class of 2025 as they step forward to shape the future with purpose, progress, and global impact.
