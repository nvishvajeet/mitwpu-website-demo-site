MIT-WPU Hosts Viksit Bharat Ambassador-Yuva Connect Programme, Inspiring Youth for Nation-Building

MIT‑WPU hosts the Viksit Bharat Ambassador Yuva Connect programme, inspiring youth for nation building through leadership and dialogue.

MIT World Peace University proudly hosted the Viksit Bharat Ambassador - Yuva Connect Program, a national initiative aimed at channeling the energy and potential of India's youth toward building a developed Bharat by 2047. The programme was launched at Kothrud campus by Shri. Anurag Singh Thakur, Member of Parliament and former Minister for Information and Broadcasting, and Youth Affairs and Sports.

In his thought-provoking address, Shri. Thakur urged the youth to take an active role in shaping the nation’s future, emphasizing the transformative progress India has already made on the journey toward becoming Viksit Bharat. He outlined a vision for 2047, encouraging students to contribute meaningfully to the nation’s development agenda.

A key highlight of the event was the planting of a sapling by Shri. Thakur as part of the 'Ek Ped Maa ke Naam' campaign, symbolizing the vital connection between environmental sustainability and national growth.

This event, part of a broader government outreach programme, saw enthusiastic participation from MIT-WPU students and the leadership team, showcasing MIT-WPU’s commitment to fostering youth engagement in nation-building. Programmes like these are integral to empowering young minds and preparing them to play a pivotal role in the development of our country, contributing to a stronger, more vibrant democracy.
