Programme

M.Des. User Experience Design

Take design innovation to the next level with MIT-WPU’s M.Des. in User Experience Design . Attain mastery in user interface design, information design, usability engineering, and much more.

Programme Overview

The M.Des. (User Experience Design) programme at MIT-WPU is an advanced and immersive journey into the world of design innovation. Building on the foundational principles of user experience design, this programme takes a deep dive into transdisciplinary design, design elements, product-service-system design, design tools, and advanced programming languages. It also explores visual design, user interface design, information design, data visualisation, storytelling, and usability engineering. At its core, this programme is forward-looking, emphasising emerging trends in artificial intelligence technologies, the internet of things, and machine learning. It fosters a keen understanding of design principles and human behaviour across a wide spectrum of industries, from information technology to healthcare, manufacturing, retail, education, and beyond.

Through a blend of hands-on projects, internships, live assignments, and study tours, students cultivate independent thinking, creativity, and innovation. This research-driven programme delves into sensory, perceptual-cognitive, behavioural, and socio-cultural domains with a strong focus on functional aesthetics. Students of this programme emerge with the ability to develop prototypes, conduct holistic research, and navigate the design development process, ultimately leading to heightened customer satisfaction. The emphasis on entrepreneurship and creativity is further bolstered through industry internships, live projects, and both national and international study tours, making students well-equipped to shape the future of user experience design.

Eligibility

Minimum 60% aggregate marks in 4-year graduation degree in Architecture/ Design/ Engineering/ Science from a UGC-approved institution/ university or its equivalent (minimum 55% aggregate marks for candidates belonging to the Reserved Category from Maharashtra State).

Selection Process

Admissions will be based on the merit of MIT-WPU CET 2026 Entrance Examination, along with Personal Interaction (PI) and Portfolio Review, conducted by MIT-WPU as per the prescribed schedule. A valid score in CEED 2026 will also be considered for the selection process; in such cases, the candidate must additionally appear for the Personal Interaction (PI) and Portfolio Review conducted by MIT-WPU as per the prescribed schedule.

Programme Outcomes

  • Cognitive Comprehension and Analysis (Understanding): Students will demonstrate an advanced understanding of human cognition and behaviour, analysing user motivations and expectations to inform intuitive and user-centred designs.
  • Innovative UXD (Creating): Students will create innovative UXDs that engage users through novel approaches, employing creativity and critical thinking to redefine user experiences.
  • Strategic Design Thinking (Applying): Students will apply design thinking strategies to complex challenges, integrating user research, empathy, and ideation to solve real-world problems.
  • Ethical and Inclusive Design (Evaluating): Students will evaluate designs from an ethical and inclusive perspective, assessing cultural sensitivities, accessibility, and social impact to ensure equitable user experiences.
  • User-Centred Research (Analysing): Students will analyse user data using qualitative and quantitative research methods, deriving actionable insights to drive informed design decisions.
  • Cross-Functional Collaboration (Collaborating): Students will collaborate effectively with multidisciplinary teams, utilising communication and interpersonal skills to translate design visions into cohesive products.
  • Technological Proficiency (Applying): Students will apply technical proficiency in industry-standard tools and technologies, integrating software skills with design principles to create functional prototypes.
  • User Interface Aesthetics (Creating): Students will create aesthetically pleasing user interfaces, harmonising visual elements and design principles to deliver engaging and visually impactful experiences.
  • Iterative Design Refinement (Analysing): Students will critically analyse user feedback and data, refining designs iteratively to enhance usability, functionality, and overall user satisfaction.
  • Strategic Leadership and Entrepreneurship (Evaluating): Students will evaluate the strategic impact of design decisions, demonstrating leadership and entrepreneurial skills to drive innovative design solutions and business initiatives.
