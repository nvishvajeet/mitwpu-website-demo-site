Programme Overview

The M.Sc. in Clinical Psychology programme at MIT-WPU in association with Vishwa Raj Hospital and the Maharashtra Institute of Medical Education and Research is a highly respected and comprehensive programme that provides students with the knowledge and skills necessary to become successful clinical psychologists. The programme is designed to prepare students for careers in a variety of settings, including hospitals, mental health clinics, and private practice.

The programme features a rigorous curriculum that covers a wide range of topics, including abnormal psychology, cognitive-behavioral therapy, and psychopharmacology. Students will also gain hands-on experience through clinical internships and research projects, allowing them to apply their knowledge in real-world settings.

Throughout the programme, students will benefit from the expertise of experienced faculty members and practitioners, who are committed to helping students succeed. Additionally, the programme's partnership with Vishwa Raj Hospital and the Maharashtra Institute of Medical Education and Research provides students with access to state-of-the-art facilities and resources, as well as opportunities for hands-on learning and collaboration with leading professionals in the field.

Overall, the M.Sc. in Clinical Psychology programme at MIT-WPU in association with Vishwa Raj Hospital and the Maharashtra Institute of Medical Education and Research is an excellent choice for students who are passionate about helping others and want to make a difference in the field of mental health.

Eligibility

Minimum 50% aggregate marks in 3/4-year graduation degree in Clinical Psychology or Life Science or allied fields (B. Pharm., MBBS, BDS, BPT, BHMS, BAMS) or Bachelor of Social Work (BSW), B.Sc. Home Science, BA Psychology, BA Sociology, B.A. Philosophy or B.Ed. from UGC-approved institution/university (Minimum 45% aggregate marks for candidates belonging to the Reserved Category from Maharashtra State).

Programme Outcomes

  • Acquire comprehensive knowledge of clinical psychology, including abnormal psychology, cognitive-behavioral therapy, psychopharmacology, and psychological assessment, preparing graduates for diverse mental health settings.
  • Develop strong practical skills through clinical internships and research projects, enabling students to apply theoretical concepts in real-world scenarios and collaborate with healthcare professionals.
  • Demonstrate proficiency in psychological testing, diagnosis, and evidence-based intervention strategies for a wide range of psychological disorders and mental health conditions.
  • Cultivate empathy, ethical responsibility, and effective communication skills essential for working with individuals, families, and communities facing psychological challenges.
  • Engage in research and critical analysis, contributing to advancements in mental health practices and policies.
  • Prepare for professional roles in hospitals, mental health clinics, private practice, rehabilitation centers, educational institutions, and research organisations.
  • Foster lifelong learning and adaptability to emerging trends and best practices in clinical psychology.
