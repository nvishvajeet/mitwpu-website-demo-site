Programme Overview

The M.Tech in Civil Engineering with specialisation in Tunnel Engineering at MIT-WPU provides students with advanced knowledge of technologies, machinery, software, and equipment used in tunnel engineering, covering fields such as tunnelling, underground construction, and geotechnical engineering. Theoretical curriculum along with practical hands-on learning prepares students for careers in the design, construction, and management of tunnels and underground infrastructure. The programme offers 100% internship opportunities in various national projects, such as the Atal Tunnel in Jammu. Special international workshops with esteemed professors offer practical exposure. The field of tunnel engineering is essential for the development of transportation, infrastructure, and underground facilities, opening up diverse and rewarding career opportunities for graduates.

Eligibility

Minimum 50% aggregate marks in a 4- year graduation degree in the relevant Engineering branch* from a UGCapproved institution/ university or its equivalent (Minimum 45% aggregate marks for candidates belonging to the Reserved Category from Maharashtra State).

Selection Process

Admission will be based on the merit of the MIT-WPU CET 2026 Entrance Examination, along with a Personal Interaction (PI) score conducted by MIT-WPU as per the prescribed schedule.

A valid score in GATE score (2026/ 2025/ 2024), PERA-CET 2026 will also be considered for the selection process; in such cases, the candidate must additionally appear for the Personal Interaction (PI) Score conducted by MIT-WPU as per the prescribed schedule.

For admission under sponsored category, candidate must have a minimum of two years of full-time work experience in a registered firm, company, industry, educational/ research institute, Government Department, or Government Autonomous Organization in the relevant field in which admission is sought. A Sponsorship Certificate (in the prescribed format) is mandatory for admission under this category.

* For more information regarding programme-wise branch eligibility, please refer to the M.Tech. rules published on the official website.

Programme Outcomes

  • Apply advanced knowledge of mathematics, science, and engineering to solve complex problems in tunnel engineering, demonstrating proficiency in specialised areas.
  • Analyse and design solutions for tunnelling projects, considering public health, safety, and environmental factors, and effectively communicate findings through technical reports and presentations.
  • Independently conduct research and contribute to the field of tunnel engineering, demonstrating a higher level of mastery beyond undergraduate requirements.
  • Adapt to and contribute to the advancements in tunnel engineering, considering economic, social, ethical, and environmental factors.
  • Exhibit strong ethical and professional responsibility, adhere to quality standards, and recognise the importance of lifelong learning for professional development in tunnel engineering.

FAQs

Graduates can pursue careers in the design, construction, and management of underground structures with organisations like DMRC, NHIDCL, NHAI, and consulting firms specialising in infrastructure projects.

The research focuses on tunnel design optimisation, advanced construction techniques, risk assessment in underground construction, ground improvement methods, and sustainable tunnelling practices.

The programme combines core theoretical curriculum with hands-on laboratory sessions, industry visits to active tunnel projects, and a mandatory 6-month dissertation project with industry collaboration.

Students gain proficiency in specialised software including PLAXIS, FLAC3D, RocScience Suite, and BIM tools specifically configured for underground construction modelling and analysis.

The curriculum covers modern approaches to energy-efficient tunnel systems, low-carbon construction materials, groundwater protection techniques, and excavation methods that minimise environmental impact.]
