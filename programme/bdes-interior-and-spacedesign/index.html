Programme Overview

This uniquely designed B.Des. (Interior and Space Design) programme at MIT-WPU will provide you with a strong foundation in the principles of interior design, while also embracing the latest trends in this ever-evolving field. The curriculum integrates residential, office, healthcare, retail, and event spaces, equipping you with skills in functional aesthetics and practical insight to create engaging, user-focused spaces. With a focus on sustainability, smart home technology, and environmental psychology, this programme trains you to design inclusive and accessible spaces that adapt to the changing needs of modern life.

The programme also exposes you to advanced technologies such as virtual reality and green building practices, enabling you to work with 3D modelling, digital rendering, and material selection. Applied learning through internships and industry collaborations ensures that you are job-ready upon graduation. The curriculum incorporates research-grounded design thinking, allowing you to understand the psychological and sensory impacts of space.

Major Tracks

  • Digital Drawing
  • Retail Design
  • Model Making
  • Spatial Ergonomics
  • Visual Merchandising

  • Early Exposure with Industry : Opportunities for internships and co-design proposals with top design studios.
  • Domain & Disciplinary Integration : You will get an all-encompassing understanding of space design, with the curriculum combining architecture, environmental psychology and design technology.

Programme Outcomes

  • Use your understanding of space planning, material technology, user psychology and building services to inform design projects that fulfil user requirements.
  • Transform human experiences and behaviour into spaces that shape and adapt according to ever-changing user needs.
  • Manage multi-disciplinary projects, acting as a team member or lead.
  • Articulate with a range of stakeholders to document, deliver, and implement design solutions.
  • Design interiors that solve social, health, safety, cultural, and legislative challenges.
  • Apply principles of sustainable design, while considering the environmental and social impact of your work.
  • Show ethical and professional responsibilities within the design profession.
  • Focus on lifelong learning and continuous technological advancement in interior and space design.
  • Develop your entrepreneurial skills by learning about the inner workings of a business in the interior design field, so you can prepare to launch and run your own design firm.
