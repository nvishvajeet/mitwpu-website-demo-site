The M.Tech in Computer Science & Engineering (Data Centre Systems Engineering) at MIT-WPU is an advanced postgraduate curriculum developed in exclusive partnership with Nirvaa Solutions Pvt. Ltd . , a global leader in data centre technology and services. This groundbreaking programme allows students to step into the rapidly expanding world of cloud computing, offering the world’s first fully industry-dedicated master's specialization of its kind.

Driven by AI architectures, enterprise cloud migrations, and 5G rollouts, the data centre sector is growing at 13.6% annually, triggering an acute national deficit of over 40,000 multidisciplinary infrastructure professionals. While traditional academic streams train students in strict engineering silos, this modern program bridges the gap by converging computer science, mechanical cooling, critical power grids, and infrastructure automation. Students build deep theoretical frameworks and practical competencies during the first year, before transitioning into a guaranteed, full-time, year-long corporate internship and live capstone project at leading industry organizations.

Eligibility

Minimum 50% aggregate marks in a 4- year graduation degree in the relevant Engineering branch* from a UGCapproved institution/ university or its equivalent (Minimum 45% aggregate marks for candidates belonging to the Reserved Category from Maharashtra State).

Selection Process

Admission will be based on the merit of the MIT-WPU CET 2026 Entrance Examination, along with a Personal Interaction (PI) score conducted by MIT-WPU as per the prescribed schedule.

A valid score in GATE score (2026/ 2025/ 2024), PERA-CET 2026 will also be considered for the selection process; in such cases, the candidate must additionally appear for the Personal Interaction (PI) Score conducted by MIT-WPU as per the prescribed schedule.

For admission under sponsored category, candidate must have a minimum of two years of full-time work experience in a registered firm, company, industry, educational/ research institute, Government Department, or Government Autonomous Organization in the relevant field in which admission is sought. A Sponsorship Certificate (in the prescribed format) is mandatory for admission under this category.

* For more information regarding programme-wise branch eligibility, please refer to the M.Tech. rules published on the official website.

Programme Highlights

  • World’s First Dedicated Curriculum: The only M.Tech programme globally offering a dedicated specialization in Data Centre Systems Engineering.
  • Industry Co-Delivery: Co-developed and co-taught directly by experienced MIT-WPU professors and industry engineers from Nirvaa Solutions Pvt. Ltd .
  • Guaranteed Year-Long Internship: The entire second year consists of a guaranteed full-time industry internship and live project deployment.

  • 100% Placement Assistance: Backed by a powerful industry partnership ecosystem offering pathways to leading global firms.

Programme Outcomes

  • Synthesize IT & Infrastructure Operations: Bridge the disappearing line between software platforms and physical facilities, effectively coordinating both IT systems and operational technologies (OT) like critical power grids and cooling units.
  • Design Redundant Data Facilities: Architect complete Tier II to Tier IV enterprise server facilities from scratch, following global structural standards, hot/cold containment logic, and failure mitigation strategies.
  • Deploy Hyperscale Cloud Solutions: Build, test, and manage large-scale multi-cloud production environments on dominant public platforms like AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.
  • Automate Infrastructure via AI: Program intelligent automation workflows and leverage machine learning to predict facility equipment degradation, reduce baseline carbon footprints, and lower net Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE).
  • Govern Security & Compliance Frameworks: Enforce robust cyber and physical protective architectures that safeguard mission-critical data assets against complex network exploits or physical security breaches.
