B.Pharm (2nd Year Lateral Entry) After 2 Years Pharmacy Diploma

Already have a pharmacy diploma? Step directly into B.Pharm 2nd year at MIT WPU Pune. PCI approved lateral entry with strong placement support. Apply now.

Programme Overview

The B.Pharm (2nd Year Lateral Entry) programme at MIT-WPU offers a unique opportunity for diploma holders in Pharmacy to directly enrol in the second year of the B.Pharm programme. This allows you to advance your knowledge and skills without needing to repeat introductory courses. The curriculum focuses on essential areas such as drug development, manufacturing, regulatory affairs, and quality assurance, opening doors to multiple opportunities within the pharmaceutical industry.

The programme combines advanced topics in pharmaceutics, medicinal chemistry, pharmacology, pharmaceutical engineering, and regulatory affairs, with practical classes and online research opportunities. You will gain insights into industry-specific subjects like quality control, drug formulation, and packaging, while also gaining hands-on experience through internships at hospitals, pharmacy chains, and research laboratories.

While the programme provides a solid theoretical foundation, the emphasis on industry exposure ensures that you are well-prepared for the job market. In addition to academic knowledge, peace studies modules contribute to your holistic development, ensuring you approach your career with ethical awareness and socio-economic consciousness. MIT-WPU’s strong ties with leading pharmaceutical companies provide excellent career opportunities in pharmaceutical manufacturing, drug research, quality control, and more.

Eligibility

Minimum 50% aggregate in D.Pharm course (45% for reservation category and persons with disability) from an institution approved by the Pharmacy Council of India under Section 12 of the Pharmacy Act.

Selection Process

The selection process for the programmes is based on Diploma scores and a Statement of Purpose (500 words) written by the student. (Provisional Admissions are offered based on 5th semester scores, in case results of final semester are awaited)

Programme Highlights

  • Comprehensive Curriculum : A focus on clinical pharmacy and pharmaceutical care. Expertise in pharmaceutical sciences and clinical practices. To help students acquire expertise on the aspects of drug therapy and patient care.
  • Innovative Pedagogy : Learning that includes critical thinking, problem-solving and adaptability, emotional intelligence, and creativity.
  • Clinical & Practical Training : Practical training at Vishwaraj Hospital of the University (In-depth hands-on experience in patient management, greater understanding of products, disease state, and the enhanced ability to problem-solve clinically).
  • Global to Local Perspective : Participate in National and Rural Immersion Programmes
  • Adverse Drug Reaction Monitoring Centre (PvPI) : Programme is recognised by the Indian Pharmacopoeia Commission. Make use of high-quality laboratories for advanced research and applied training.
  • Industry Collaborations and Career Support : Robust links to industry for placements, internships, and working together with pharmaceutical firms and health services.

FAQs

Applicants who have done a 2-year Diploma in Pharmacy can apply for B.Pharm Lateral Entry, which allows seeking admission in the second year of the B.Pharm programme. It saves you time and lets you continue without having to repeat introductory courses. This program helps you enhance and leverage your prior knowledge and pursue senior roles in the pharmaceutical sector.

Transfer is assessed on an individual basis and you must also satisfy the programme’s entry and academic criteria.

  • Critical Knowledge : Understanding about latest improvements in pharmaceuticals, like drug development and excellent management.
