Programme Overview

Explore a multi-dimensional career as a visual artist with a Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA) in Applied Arts at MIT-WPU. This course places a strong emphasis on hands-on experience, innovative problem-solving, and the practical application of knowledge, preparing you for a dynamic career in fields such as advertising, branding, graphic design, e-learning, and more. The curriculum covers a wide range of topics, including graphic design, typography, illustration, packaging design, and photography, alongside both theoretical and practical training. Additionally, students have the opportunity to gain extra certifications through MOOCs, enhancing their industry-readiness.

The programme places significant emphasis on real-world experience, exposing you to current marketplace trends through internships, live projects, and industry visits. These opportunities are designed to prepare you for the ever-evolving creative field. Special modules like Peace Studies promote holistic development and equip you with essential skills, such as teamwork, communication, and leadership, which are vital for success in any profession.

The BFA in Applied Arts programme trains graduates to craft impactful marketing campaigns, produce original content, and build compelling brand identities. With a well-rounded skill set combining both creative and technical expertise, you will graduate ready to thrive as an innovative practitioner in a creative agency or through freelance projects, contributing to the rapidly growing applied arts and media industries.

Eligibility

Minimum 50% aggregate marks in Class 10+2/ HSC or its equivalent examination in any stream with English subject (Minimum 45% aggregate marks for candidates belonging to the Reserved Category from Maharashtra State).

Selection Process

Admissions will be based purely on the merit of MIT-WPU CET 2026 Personal Interaction (PI), Portfolio Review, and Drawing Test by a Jury. The Portfolio Review and Drawing Test will be exempted if the candidate has a valid score in UCEED/PERA/MAH-CET (Applied Arts); however, the candidate must additionally appear for the MIT-WPU CET Personal Interaction (PI) as per the schedule.
