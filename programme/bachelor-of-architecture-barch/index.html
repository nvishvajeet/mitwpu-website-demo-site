Programme Overview

The flagship programme at the Hafeez Contractor School of Architecture is the Bachelor of Architecture (B.Arch.) , a five-year full-time professional degree designed to develop future-ready architects.

The curriculum is strictly governed by the Council of Architecture (CoA) and aligned with NEP 2020 , ensuring a balance of design excellence, technical expertise, and ethical practice. The programme follows a studio-centric pedagogy , where learning is immersive, hands-on, and project-driven.

The academic journey is structured in phases:

  • Foundation Years : Building core design, visualisation, and conceptual skills
  • Core Professional Years : Integration of structure, services, sustainability, and technology
  • Final Phase :Mandatory practical training (internship) and a research-driven architectural thesis

With a strong focus on digital mastery, sustainability, and interdisciplinary collaboration, the programme prepares students to lead in a rapidly evolving built environment.

Programme Objectives (POs)

Architecture Graduates will be able to:

  • PO-1 : Architectural Knowledge : Apply the knowledge of humanities, arts, building sciences, and technology fundamentals to the solution of complex design problems.
  • PO-2 :Problem Analysis: Identify and analyze complex spatial, urban, and socio- cultural problems reaching substantiated design conclusions using research and analysis.
  • PO-3 : Design/Development of Solutions: Design holistic solutions for the built environment that meet specified aesthetic and functional needs with due consideration for public safety, culture, and ecology.
  • PO-4 : Investigations of Design Challenges: Use research-based knowledge to conduct site analysis, user-behavior studies, and data synthesis to provide valid design interventions.
  • PO-5 : Modern Tool Usage: Select and apply appropriate digital techniques (BIM, Parametric tools, Simulation software) and modern construction technologies with an understanding of their limitations.
  • PO-6 : The Architect and Society: Apply reasoning informed by contextual knowledge to assess societal, health, safety, and legal issues relevant to professional architectural practice.
  • PO-7 : Environment and Sustainability: Understand the impact of architectural solutions in societal and environmental contexts and demonstrate the need for net-zero and sustainable development.
  • PO-8 : Ethics: Apply ethical principles and commit to professional ethics and the Code of Conduct prescribed by the Council of Architecture (CoA).
  • PO-9 : Individual and Teamwork: Function effectively as an individual, and as a member or leader in diverse design teams and multidisciplinary settings.
  • PO-10 : Communication: Communicate effectively on complex architectural activities through visual portfolios, technical drawings, oral presentations, and written reports.
  • PO-11 : Project Management and Finance: Demonstrate knowledge of management principles, building economics, and professional practice to manage projects within multidisciplinary environments.
  • PO-12 : Life-long Learning: Recognize the need for and have the ability to engage in independent and life-long learning in the context of evolving technological and social changes.

An Architect studies a client's space requirements and the site's context, then designs spaces and structures that meet those requirements by balancing functional, technical and aesthetic considerations. Architects visualise spaces and structures and communicate their ideas through drawings, models and walkthroughs. They work closely with consultants such as structural engineers and services consultants (plumbing, drainage, electrical, HVAC) to ensure the design functions efficiently. Architects also prepare construction drawings and details, support the tendering process, and supervise construction at key stages on site. An Architect's role combines Office work (design, drawing, and consultant coordination) with Field work (site studies and supervision).

Architecture graduates have strong career prospects and can pursue many paths beyond classical practice. Popular specialisations for further study and practice include Urban Design, Landscape Architecture, Architectural Conservation, Building Engineering and Management, Digital Architecture, Environmental Architecture, Interior Design, Sustainable Design, Housing, Parametric Design, Urban Planning, Environmental Planning, Construction Management, Traditional Indian Knowledge Systems, Architectural History, Transportation Planning, Computer Applications, and Energy-efficient Architecture, among others. Beyond classical practice or working at architecture firms, many graduates also build careers in academics and research.

After completing the degree, graduates must register with the Council of Architecture (COA), the statutory body responsible for issuing the license to practice.

The curriculum includes two categories: subjects mandated by the Council of Architecture (COA) and courses unique to MIT-WPU, Pune, that offer transdisciplinary learning.

COA-prescribed courses include : (1) Professional Core (PC) Courses, such as Architectural Design, History of Architecture, Landscape Design, Urban Design, Site Planning, Architectural Drawing and Graphics, and Model Making.

(2) Building Sciences and Applied Engineering (BS and AE) Courses, such as Building Materials and Construction, Theory of Structures, Building Services, Climatology, Environmental Studies, and Surveying and Levelling.

(3) Elective Courses of two types: Professional Electives (PE), such as Vernacular Architecture, Interior Design, Sustainable Cities and Disaster Management; and Open Electives (OE), covering subjects beyond architecture that broaden a student's overall development.

(4) Employability Enhancement Courses (EEC) of two kinds: Employability Enhancement Compulsory Courses (EECC), such as Professional Practice and Internship; and Skill Enhancement Courses (SEC), such as Communication Skills and Entrepreneurship Skills.

In addition, MIT-WPU, Pune, offers credit courses including Financial Literacy, Indian Knowledge Systems, AI for All, Foundations of Peace, Co-creation, Rural and National Immersion, and Yoga, among others.

The programme runs for a minimum of 5 academic years (10 semesters), each spanning 15 to 18 working weeks (90 workdays). This includes one semester of practical training (about 16 working weeks) in the 8th or 9th semester, as prescribed under the COA's Minimum Standards of Architectural Education Regulations 2020.

Architectural education at MIT-WPU, Pune, is student-centric and studio-based, with emphasis on active rather than passive learning. Teaching methods include: 1. Lectures: structured sessions covering foundational concepts and the methodology of scientific work. 2. Seminars: interactive discussions between faculty and students. 3. Labs/Workshops: hands-on delivery of course content. 4. Studio exercises: faculty guide individual or group problem-solving tasks. 5. Internship: students train under COA-registered architects at architecture firms or government departments. 6. Study tours and site visits: expose students to professional practice as well as the culture and context of a region.

A merit list is prepared using NATA scores (50% weightage) and HSC qualifying exam scores (50% weightage). Applicants must also submit a 500-word Statement of Purpose (SOP) and a portfolio of their creative work (manual or digital).
