Programme Overview

The MBA programme in International Business at MIT World Peace University is skillfully designed by industry experts to equip students for prosperous careers in global business management. The goal of this comprehensive programme is to enable graduates with the industry knowledge and perspective required to attain international success.

The curriculum is designed to give students knowledge about the business principles and global industry perspective which enables them to navigate complex business problems across borders.

The programme features a wide variety of subjects designed to deepen your understanding of cross-cultural management, international trade and analytics, business practices, marketing strategies, global finance, and strategic management.

MBA IB helps in attaining essential business skills such as critical thinking, problem-solving, negotiation, and leadership, crucial for making informed decisions in a global context.

Eligibility

Minimum 60% aggregate marks in 3/ 4- year graduation degree in any stream from a UGC-approved institution/ university or its equivalent (Minimum 55% aggregate marks for candidates belonging to the Reserved Category from Maharashtra State). AND A minimum of 50% aggregate marks in Class 10 (SSC) and Class 12 (HSC) examinations, with English as a compulsory subject. AND A valid non-zero score in the relevant entrance examination conducted by the competent authority.

Selection Process

Admission will be based on the merit of a valid score in CAT 2025, XAT 2026, NMAT 2025, GMAT, CMAT 2026, MAH-CET 2026, PERA 2026 or MAT (September 2025 onwards). AND Candidates will also be evaluated through 50 marks online CBT, Group Discussion (GD), Extempore, Personal Interaction (PI) score conducted by MIT-WPU as per the prescribed schedule, along with consideration of their Past Academic Record and Work Experience. AND The valid (non-zero) national level entrance score is mandatory for confirmation of admission.

Programme Highlights

  • Dynamic Case-Based Learning : The curriculum emphasises practical application through real-world case studies.
  • Internal Competence for Outer Demand (ICODs) : This initiative develops core capabilities to tackle real-world challenges.
  • Focused Start-Up Mentoring : Students receive support to cultivate entrepreneurial skills and innovative projects.
  • Industry Exposure : Graduates get industry exposure and hands-on experience through various site visits, internships, and live projects.
  • Dedicated Support Structures : Our Centre for Industry-Academia Partnerships (CIAP) is committed to providing ongoing assistance to students in getting internships and job placements.
  • Curriculum Alignment : The curriculum follows Bloom's Taxonomy and the LOCF Model to ensure comprehensive learning outcomes.
  • Cultural Immersion Programmes : Students engage in rural, national, and international immersion experiences to understand diverse business contexts.
  • Advisory Board Insights : Guidance from an advisory board composed of national and international experts enriches student learning.
  • Guest Lectures : Interactions with management experts and international faculty enhance educational experiences.

Programme Outcomes

  • Comprehensive knowledge about complexities of international business theoretically and practically to address future challenges.
  • Evaluate foreign market entry strategies and global supply chain management to draw conclusions based on international business standards.
  • Develop effective international business strategies considering cultural, economic, and geopolitical factors.
  • Research-based methodologies to investigate complex issues within the dynamic landscape of international business.
  • Assess the societal and ethical implications of international business decisions while promoting sustainable practices.
  • Understand how international business solutions impact global society and contribute to sustainable development.
  • Uphold ethical standards in international business practices while demonstrating accountability.
  • Collaborate effectively with diverse teams across multicultural environments.
  • Convey complex business concepts clearly to both specialised and general audiences, employing effective cross-cultural communication skills.
  • Implementing international business management principles on project management and strategic decision-making processes to drive organisational success

FAQs

MBA IB is a full time two year degree programme that equips students with fundamentals of international business management, global industry perspective,navigate complex business problems and cross border relationships.

Studying for an MBA in International Business provides multiple advantages:

  • Global Exposure
  • Networking Opportunities
  • Internship Potential
  • Enhanced Adaptability

Students will acquire essential skills including:

  • Strategic Thinking
  • Leadership Skills
  • Analytical Skills
  • Cross-Cultural Communication

Graduates can pursue various roles such as:

  • Global Business Manager
  • Export Manager
  • Investment Banker
  • International Marketing Consultant

Preparation involves focusing on:

  • Quantitative Aptitude
  • Logical Reasoning
  • Verbal Ability
  • Taking practice tests and considering previous year question papers can also be beneficial.

The MBA in International Business is a two year postgraduate programme and is divided into four semesters. The first year covers core management subjects, while the second year focuses on specialised subjects and electives.
