Programme Overview

The B.Tech Civil Engineering at MIT-WPU is a focused programme designed to develop professionals equipped with both practical and theoretical knowledge of civil engineering. Recognised for its comprehensive educational scope, the programme is tailored to address modern infrastructure challenges. It offers a holistic understanding of physical infrastructure and construction processes by blending traditional engineering foundations with cutting-edge digital technologies such as IoT, AI, and data analytics.

This programme provides hands-on experience through projects and case studies, enabling graduates to effectively deliver infrastructure solutions. Its detailed curriculum fosters an adaptable mindset, preparing students for an industry characterised by rapid transformations. At MIT-WPU, students will also develop creativity and leadership skills essential for excelling in the dynamic field of civil engineering.

Eligibility

A minimum of 50% aggregate marks in Class 10+2/ HSC, with English as a compulsory subject and A minimum of 50% aggregate marks in any one of the following subject groups:

  • PCM (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics),
  • Physics & Mathematics with any Technical Vocational Subject
  • PCB* (Physics, Chemistry, Biology*),

[*Biology/Biotechnology is applicable only for the Bioengineering Programme.] (Minimum 45% aggregate marks for candidates belonging to the Reserved Category from Maharashtra State). OR A minimum 60% aggregate marks in a Diploma in Engineering & Technology in a relevant branch from a government-approved technical board.

Selection Process

Admissions are based on the scores of JEE 2026/ MHT-CET 2026/ PERA-CET 2026, or MHT-CET-B* 2026/ NEET 2026* (*applicable only for the Bioengineering programme), along with a 500-word Statement of Purpose (SOP) written by the candidate

Programme Highlights

  • The programme combines both practical and theoretical learning, with a strong emphasis on ethics.
  • MIT-WPU collaborates with industry leaders such as PCERF, BAI, and KL Structures USA to provide industry-aligned education.
  • Research interests include sustainable structure design and advanced earthquake response techniques.
  • International collaborations with renowned institutions, such as Burton and BSDC in the UK, provide global exposure.
  • State-of-the-art laboratories support applied learning across disciplines, including surveying, cement testing, and environmental engineering.
  • The project-based approach integrates theory and practice, equipping you with the skills to address real-world challenges effectively.

FAQs

No, MIT-WPU does not conduct a separate entrance exam for B.Tech admissions. Admissions are granted based on the candidate’s performance in any of the following national/state-level entrance exams: NEET 2026/ MHT-CET 2026/ JEE 2026/ PERA-CET 2026 (applicable only for the Bioengineering programme.) Candidates must have a valid score in any of the above exams to be considered for admission.

MIT-WPU’s CIAP (Centre for Industry-Academia Partnerships), helps students engage in industry-supported projects, internships with leading companies, and hands-on training in civil engineering concepts.

Students are taught to use industry-standard software such as AutoCAD, SAP2000, STAAD Pro, Revit, and GIS tools. Proficiency in these tools is essential for modern civil engineering practices.

B.E. Civil Engineering : Focuses on theoretical principles and is research-oriented, suitable for academic or research roles. B.Tech Civil Engineering : Emphasises practical, industry-relevant skills, preparing you for diverse roles in civil engineering industries.

The programme incorporates technologies like IoT, AI, Big Data, and data visualisation to enable innovative and sustainable infrastructure solutions.
