BA / B.Sc. (Honours / Honours with Research)

Study BA Liberal Arts at MIT WPU Pune. Interdisciplinary curriculum, global faculty and research pathways at a QS I GAUGE Diamond rated university.

Programme Overview

The BA/B.Sc. (Honours/Honours with Research) with Liberal Arts Pedagogy programme at MIT-WPU's School of Liberal Arts offers a transformative educational experience that combines breadth of knowledge with depth of expertise. Designed to foster critical thinking, creativity, and ethical leadership, these programmes prepare students to navigate complex global challenges through a transdisciplinary approach.

Students can choose from 6 majors and 16 minors, creating over 96 unique combinations tailored to their interests and career aspirations. The curriculum integrates humanities, social sciences, and behavioural sciences, emphasizing experiential learning through rural and global immersion programs, internships, and research projects.

With flexible pathways aligned with NEP 2020, students can graduate with a 3-year BA/B.Sc. with Minor or pursue a 4-year BA/B.Sc. (Honours/Honours with Research), gaining the skills and knowledge needed for diverse careers or advanced studies.

Major and Minor Tracks

Major Tracks

  • Psychology (BA) : Explore human behaviour, cognition, and mental processes through scientific inquiry and applied practice.
  • Business Psychology (B.Sc.) : Apply psychological principles to business contexts, including organizational behaviour, consumer psychology, and workplace dynamics.
  • Literary and Communication Studies (BA) : Master the art of storytelling, critical analysis, and effective communication across diverse media platforms.
  • Politics and Global Affairs (BA) : Analyse political systems, international relations, and global governance to understand and shape world events.
  • Public Policy (BA) : Design, evaluate, and implement policies that address pressing social, economic, and environmental challenges.
  • Sociology (BA) : Examine social structures, cultural dynamics, and societal change to promote justice and equity.

Minor Tracks

  • Psychology : Understand human behaviour, mental processes, and psychological principles to complement any major.
  • Literary and Communication Studies : Develop advanced writing, communication, and critical analysis skills across various media.
  • Politics and Global Affairs : Gain insights into political systems, governance, and international relations.
  • Public Policy : Learn policy analysis, design, and evaluation to address societal challenges.
  • Sociology : Study social structures, cultural dynamics, and contemporary social issues.
  • Criminology : Explore criminal behaviour, justice systems, and crime prevention strategies.
  • Anthropology : Examine human cultures, societies, and evolutionary perspectives on human diversity.
  • Mass Communication : Master journalism, media production, and strategic communication for diverse platforms.
  • Film Making : Learn screenwriting, film production, editing, and visual storytelling techniques.
  • Foreign Languages : Develop proficiency in French, German, Spanish, or Mandarin with cultural competence.
  • Forensic Psychology : Apply psychological principles to legal contexts, criminal investigations, and justice systems.
  • International Relations : Understand global politics, diplomacy, international organizations, and cross-border issues.
  • Economics : Analyse economic theories, markets, development, and policy implications.
  • Cognitive Neuroscience : Explore the biological basis of cognition, brain function, and behaviour.
  • Clinical Psychology : Study psychopathology, assessment, counselling techniques, and therapeutic interventions.
  • Data Analytics : Master statistical analysis, data visualization, programming, and data-driven decision making.
  • Digital Humanities : An interdisciplinary field that uses digital tools and methods to research, analyze, preserve, and interpret human culture, history, and creative expression.
