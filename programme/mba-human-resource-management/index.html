Programme Overview

Unlock your potential and the future of work administration with MIT-WPU’s two years comprehensive programme MBA in Human Resource Management. The curriculum is designed by professionals to give students a strong foundation in business administration and specialised understanding of human resources.

The programme helps students in understanding key areas including organisational change management, talent acquisition, and conflict resolution by combining academic learning with real-world projects.

Internships and industrial projects play a pivotal role in the academic journey, as they provide practical exposure to students and equip them to become well-known leaders, adept problem solvers, and change agents in the complicated workforce of today.

Graduates of the MBA in Human Resource Management programme are prepared significantly to create impact in their organisations by developing leadership abilities and addressing HR issues in a variety of industries.

Eligibility

Minimum 60% aggregate marks in 3/ 4- year graduation degree in any stream from a UGC-approved institution/ university or its equivalent (Minimum 55% aggregate marks for candidates belonging to the Reserved Category from Maharashtra State). AND A minimum of 50% aggregate marks in Class 10 (SSC) and Class 12 (HSC) examinations, with English as a compulsory subject. AND A valid non-zero score in the relevant entrance examination conducted by the competent authority.

Selection Process

Admission will be based on the merit of a valid score in CAT 2025, XAT 2026, NMAT 2025, GMAT, CMAT 2026, MAH-CET 2026, PERA 2026 or MAT (September 2025 onwards). AND Candidates will also be evaluated through 50 marks online CBT, Group Discussion (GD), Extempore, Personal Interaction (PI) score conducted by MIT-WPU as per the prescribed schedule, along with consideration of their Past Academic Record and Work Experience. AND The valid (non-zero) national level entrance score is mandatory for confirmation of admission.

Programme Outcomes

  • Apply strategic HR management knowledge to navigate the entire employee life cycle.
  • Identify, analyse, and address complex HR issues by applying critical thinking and problem-solving skills.
