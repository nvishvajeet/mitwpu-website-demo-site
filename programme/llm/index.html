Programme Overview

The LLM programme at the School of Law aims to develop strong legal research skills among the students and the ability to apply key concepts of substantive and procedural law to specific situations. Through a thorough study of society, laws, and judicial procedures, students learn to analyse and solve legal problems through logical reasoning. The programme also teaches the theories and practices of legislative enactment and interpretation, and trains students to apply these skills in simulated court sessions. Students have the option to specialise in one of four areas: constitutional law, criminal law, international law, or business law.

Programme Outcomes

  • Develop advanced legal research skills and the ability to critically analyse and apply key concepts of substantive and procedural law across specialised domains such as constitutional law, criminal law, international law, and business law.
  • Demonstrate strong written and oral communication through rigorous moot court sessions, debates, and legal writing, preparing graduates for advocacy, academia, and judicial roles.
  • Acquire a comprehensive understanding of the relationship between law, society, and judicial processes, enabling effective engagement with socio-economic and grassroots legal issues through immersion programmes and practical exposure.
  • Exhibit professional and ethical responsibility in legal practice, with skills in negotiation, counseling, and legal reasoning relevant to diverse legal careers including corporate law, public service, and legal advisory roles.
  • Apply legal knowledge to real-world challenges, contributing to policy development, legal reforms, and the advancement of justice at local, national, and international levels.
