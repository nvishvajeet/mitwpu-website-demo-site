Programme Overview

The M.Sc. in Biotechnology at MIT-WPU prepares students for careers in the healthcare, biopharmaceutical, industrial, and related sectors. The programme covers a wide range of subjects, including biochemistry, genomics, proteomics, stem cell technology, nanobiotechnology, biomimetics, bioanalytics, pharmacovigilance, computational biology and systems, and synthetic biology. The programme structure is developed in consultation with leading researchers, biomedical, clinical, and industrial experts to provide students with the specialised knowledge and practical skills needed to succeed in the biotechnology field.

As part of the programme, students complete two research projects in dedicated postgraduate labs and with leading researchers from the school and collaborating organisations. This provides students with substantial research experience and helps them develop industry and academic skills through a combination of academics, internships, and entrepreneurial opportunities.

Programme Outcomes

  • Acquire advanced knowledge across key biotechnology domains, including biochemistry, genomics, proteomics, stem cell technology, nanobiotechnology, biomimetics, bioanalytics, pharmacovigilance, computational biology, and systems and synthetic biology.
  • Develop specialised laboratory and research skills through completion of two research projects in dedicated postgraduate labs, working alongside leading researchers and collaborating organisations.
  • Gain practical experience and industry readiness via internships, hands-on training, and exposure to real-world biotechnological challenges in healthcare, biopharmaceutical, and industrial sectors.
  • Demonstrate the ability to analyse, evaluate, and disseminate biotechnology research, and apply expertise to solve problems in diverse fields such as health, agriculture, environment, and industry.
  • Cultivate professional competencies in scientific communication, teamwork, ethical practices, and effective project management, preparing graduates for roles in research, academia, industry, and entrepreneurship.
  • Be equipped for a wide range of career paths, including positions as biotechnologists, research scientists, quality assurance technologists, biomedical research assistants, environmental microbiologists, and biotechnology business consultants.
