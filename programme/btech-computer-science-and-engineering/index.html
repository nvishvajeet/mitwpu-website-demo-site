Programme Overview

The MIT-WPU B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering programme combines foundational computer science principles with cutting-edge technologies like Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, Cyber Security, and Blockchain. It integrates theoretical knowledge with practical applications, providing a comprehensive understanding of the field.

The programme prepares you to excel in this dynamic and growing discipline, offering specialised tracks in Data Science and Information & Cyber Security or Software Design & Development. With hands-on labs, industry collaborations, and real-world projects, you will be well-equipped to tackle real-world challenges and lead in the technology-driven world.

Eligibility

A minimum of 50% aggregate marks in Class 10+2/ HSC, with English as a compulsory subject and A minimum of 50% aggregate marks in any one of the following subject groups:

  • PCM (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics),
  • Physics & Mathematics with any Technical Vocational Subject
  • PCB* (Physics, Chemistry, Biology*),

[*Biology/Biotechnology is applicable only for the Bioengineering Programme.] (Minimum 45% aggregate marks for candidates belonging to the Reserved Category from Maharashtra State). OR A minimum 60% aggregate marks in a Diploma in Engineering & Technology in a relevant branch from a government-approved technical board.

Selection Process

Admissions are based on the scores of JEE 2026/ MHT-CET 2026/ PERA-CET 2026, or MHT-CET-B* 2026/ NEET 2026* (*applicable only for the Bioengineering programme), along with a 500-word Statement of Purpose (SOP) written by the candidate

FAQs

The B.Tech Computer Science and Engineering curriculum includes core subjects such as data structures, algorithms, operating systems, software engineering, computer networks, database management systems, and artificial intelligence. Additionally, niche subjects like cyber security, data science, machine learning, and blockchain equip you with skills relevant to the rapidly evolving tech industry.

B.Tech CSE has a broader scope compared to B.Tech IT, as it extensively covers core computer science areas such as programming, software development, data structures, algorithms, artificial intelligence, and machine learning. These skills can lead to roles like software developer, data scientist, or AI engineer. B.Tech IT, on the other hand, focuses more on IT infrastructure, system administration, and business management. Both offer good job opportunities, but if you aspire to work in software development, AI, or data analytics, B.Tech CSE is the better choice.

The B.Tech CSE at MIT-WPU is designed to keep students abreast of the fast-evolving AI and Machine Learning domains. The curriculum includes essential topics like machine learning algorithms, deep learning techniques, and AI computing platforms. Students work on real-world projects such as data analysis, predictive modelling, computer vision, and natural language processing. With access to state-of-the-art facilities, cutting-edge labs, and technology, the programme blends theoretical concepts with practical applications, ensuring readiness for real-world challenges in AI and ML.

  • Cutting-Edge Curriculum: The programme offers a comprehensive curriculum covering fields like AI, Data Science, Cyber Security, Blockchain, and Machine Learning.
  • Hands-on Learning: Students work on real-life projects, engage in industry collaborations, and participate in internships using emerging technologies such as AI, IoT, and Big Data.
  • Industry-Ready Skills: Gain expertise in high-demand areas like Cyber Security, Software Development, and Multimedia & Computer Vision, making you ready for diverse roles in the tech industry.
  • International Exposure and Career Support: Benefit from internship guidance with international industries, access to a 100,000+ strong alumni network, and mentoring programmes.
  • MIT-WPU Technology Business Incubator (TBI): For entrepreneurial aspirants, the TBI programme offers funding, mentorship, and networking opportunities.

B.Tech CSE vs. B.Tech IT

B.Tech CSE is more software-oriented, focusing on computer systems, programming, and development, while B.Tech IT emphasises technology, networks, and managing information systems.

B.Tech CSE vs. BCA

B.Tech CSE is a four-year engineering programme offering technical depth in computer science concepts, whereas BCA Science (Hons) is a 4 year undergraduate degree focused on programming and application development with less technical emphasis.
