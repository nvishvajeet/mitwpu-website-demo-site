M.Tech Petroleum Engineering

Build on your Petroleum Engineering knowledge by getting a Master's degree from MIT-WPU. The M.Tech Petroleum Engineering programme includes internships, workshops, and research opportunities for all students.

Programme Overview

The M.Tech in Petroleum Engineering programme at MIT-WPU is meant to upgrade students' expertise in petroleum-related fields like exploration, drilling, production, and handling of oil and gas resources.

This programme is based on the foundational knowledge from undergraduate studies and equips students with essential principles, tools, and systems for effective petroleum extraction and refining.

The curriculum is in alignment with the Society of Petroleum Engineers' standards and involves practical exposure through internships, real-time projects, and research opportunities. During the programme, the students can also choose to specialise in areas such as Subsea Engineering, Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, Geosciences and more. These electives prepare the graduates for careers in national and international oil companies, government agencies, consulting firms, and research institutions.

The programme ensures a strong technical understanding and application of the theoretical modules leading to better training and placement opportunities with leading companies like ONGC, Shell, and more. Students during the programme gain hands-on experience through case-based learning and state-of-the-art lab facilities, that enhance their problem-solving skills and professional readiness.

Eligibility

Minimum 50% aggregate marks in a 4- year graduation degree in the relevant Engineering branch* from a UGCapproved institution/ university or its equivalent (Minimum 45% aggregate marks for candidates belonging to the Reserved Category from Maharashtra State).

Selection Process

Admission will be based on the merit of the MIT-WPU CET 2026 Entrance Examination, along with a Personal Interaction (PI) score conducted by MIT-WPU as per the prescribed schedule.

A valid score in GATE score (2026/ 2025/ 2024), PERA-CET 2026 will also be considered for the selection process; in such cases, the candidate must additionally appear for the Personal Interaction (PI) Score conducted by MIT-WPU as per the prescribed schedule.

For admission under sponsored category, candidate must have a minimum of two years of full-time work experience in a registered firm, company, industry, educational/ research institute, Government Department, or Government Autonomous Organization in the relevant field in which admission is sought. A Sponsorship Certificate (in the prescribed format) is mandatory for admission under this category.

* For more information regarding programme-wise branch eligibility, please refer to the M.Tech. rules published on the official website.

FAQs

M.E. programmes generally emphasise the theoretical aspects of engineering, while M.Tech programmes, focus on practical applications and offer specialised skills relevant to the petroleum industry.

The key skills required for pursuing this programme are as follows:

  • Numeric & Analytical Skills for data analysis and problem-solving
  • Fitness for fieldwork and operational roles.
  • Proactiveness and enthusiasm for tackling the challenges in the industry.
  • Team spirit to collaborate in engineering projects.
  • Problem-solving skills for addressing complex engineering issues.

This decision depends on individual career goals. Generally pursuing an M.Tech immediately after a B.Tech enhances specialisation, while gaining work experience first provides practical insights that enrich your advanced studies.

Graduates can pursue roles such as:

  • Exploration Engineer
  • Reservoir Engineer
  • Seismic Data Acquisition Engineer
  • Processing Platform Engineer
  • Petroleum Engineer
  • Research Associate
  • Project Assistant
  • Manager or Advisor in petroleum-related sectors
