Programme Overview

The Master of Computer Applications (MCA) at MIT-WPU fosters a research-oriented mindset throughout students' academic careers, this programme seeks to strengthen students' fundamental knowledge of computer science while progressively exposing them to new developments.

Incorporating the methodologies of computer science and engineering, the programme illustrates real-world applications while fostering a research mindset. Students learn about the new tools, technologies, and applications through a concentrated curriculum that encompasses Internet technologies, machine learning, data science, mobile application development, programming languages, advanced databases, computer science fundamentals, and Agile and Scrum Master methodologies.

Industry-specific internships, real-world projects, and collaborations with businesses offer essential pre-placement exposure to practical experiences. Graduates possess the skills to successfully conceptualise, design, develop, implement, and execute software applications.

Eligibility

Minimum 60% aggregate marks in 3/4-year graduation degree in science stream/ BCAScience from a UGC-approved institution/ university or its equivalent. In addition, candidates must have studied Mathematics as one of the subjects at the Class10+2 level or at the graduate level (Minimum 55% aggregate marks for candidates belonging to the Reserved Category from Maharashtra State).

Selection Process

Admission will be based on the MIT-WPU CET 2026 Entrance Examination and the Personal Interaction (PI) score, conducted by MIT-WPU as per the prescribed schedule.

FAQs

The core subjects covered in an MCA programme are Programming in C, Data Structures, Database Management Systems, Operating Systems, Software Engineering, and Computer Networks. Other subjects may include Machine Learning, Cloud Computing, and Web Technologies.

MCA programmes curricula are aligned with the rest of the world as far as software development, data structures, and programming languages are concerned, though it can be different in terms of intensity and specific technologies used from those in the USA or UK.

An MCA programme can involve different options for specialising that may include Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, Cyber Security, Software Development, and Cloud Computing. This enables students to be able to specialise in their interests' areas.

Minimum 60% in 3-year Graduation in science stream / BCA from UGC approved Institution or its equivalent (at least 55% marks, in case of Reserved Class category candidate belonging to Maharashtra State only) and should have studied Mathematics as one of the subjects at 12th Class level or graduate level.
